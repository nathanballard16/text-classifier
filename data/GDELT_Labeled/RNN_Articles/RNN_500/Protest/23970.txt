Abuse me, but don't destroy public property: PM Modi accuses rivals of creating fear over Citizenship Amendment Act

Narendra Modi

Amid ongoing violence and protests across the country over the Citizenship (Amendment) Act (CAA), Prime Ministeron Sunday slammed the Congress and Opposition parties and accused them of creating fear over the new law.Modi made the remarks while sounding a poll bugle for the Delhi assembly elections during his address to a public rally at Ramlila Maidan.Modi began his address at the rally with the slogan 'Unity in diversity is India's speciality'.Amid the chants of Bharat Mata ki Jai, Modi urged the crowd to join him in saying, 'Vividha me ekta, Bharat ki visheshta' (Unity in diversity is India's speciality).Asking people at Delhi rally to stand up to show respect for Parliament and lawmakers who passed the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, Modi alleged that people were incited and those in high positions shared fake videos.Modi also said that amended citizenship law has nothing to do with Indian citizens and urged protesters not to burn poor's houses, their business. "What will you get from beating poor people, poor drivers and policemen.""Some political parties spreading all kinds of rumours, misleading people and stoking their emotions against citizenship law. Lie is being spread that I brought law to snatch people's rights, it will not stand," said Modi.Stating that his rivals should burn his effigy if they hate him but should not target the poor, Modi said, "I dare rivals to find anything discriminatory in my work. Muslims being misled, I have always ensured that documents will never come in way of development schemes and their beneficiaries. We have never asked anyone if he goes to temple or mosque when it comes to implementing our schemes.""Hindus, Muslims, Sikhs, Christians all were befitted, everyone who lives here was benefitted. Why did we do this? Because we live for the love of the country. We are dedicated to the mantra of 'Sabka saath, sabka vikas'."In the protests, several people have lost their lives and many have been injured.As many as 879 people have been arrested as a preventive measure in Uttar Pradesh, in the wake of the violent protests.The CAA seeks to grant citizenship to Hindus, Sikhs, Jains, Parsis, Buddhists and Christians fleeing religious persecution from Pakistan, Afghanistan and Bangladesh and came to India on or before December 31, 2014.
