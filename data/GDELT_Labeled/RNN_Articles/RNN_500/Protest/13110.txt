Indian opposition parties to send delegation to Occupied Kashmir today

A delegation of Indian opposition leaders, including former Congress President Rahul Gandhi will visit Srinagar to assess the prevailing situation in Occupied Kashmir, where curfew and restrictions have been imposed since the Centre revoked Jammu and Kashmir's special status.

Earlier, Opposition leaders met in New Delhi on Friday and decided to send the delegation to Kashmir.

The Communist Party of India's D. Raja said that the delegation is made up of recognized national leaders.

He said we are public faces, and they should not stop us. D Raja questioned that will New Delhi now pump bullets into us when we land.

Meanwhile, Leader of an Indian political party Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam, M K Stalin, held a demonstration at Jantar Mantar in New Delhi to protest against the situation in Kashmir.
