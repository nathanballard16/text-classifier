Three try to loot ATM in Jagraon

LUDHIANA: Three men tried to loot the automated teller machine (ATM) of a private bank on Heera Singh Road in Jagraon early on Tuesday.Police said around 2.50am, three men reached the Axis Bank ATM kiosk on a motorcycle. “The trio broke open the shutter of the kiosk, which remains closed from 8pm to 8am. After gaining access, they damaged its two CCTV cameras. While they were trying to open the ATM, they heard some movement in the area and fled. They could not reach the machine’s cash box,” said a cop.A CCTV camera installed at a house in the area has captured them, but not their faces.In the morning, the guard of a nearby bank noticed the broken shutter and informed the bank authorities. The bank authorities checked the ATM and found the machine’s cash box intact. They then sounded the police.SHO Nidhan Singh said, “At 2.53am, DSP Brij Mohan was briefing the PCR cops at Rani Jhansi chowk. Following his instructions, the PCR cops crossed through the area with the vehicle siren on. The looters must have fled on hearing the police siren.”A case has been registered under sections 380 (theft in dwelling house, etc), 427 (mischief causing damage of Rs 50 or more), 457 (lurking house-trespass or house-breaking by night in order to commit offence punishable with imprisonment), 511 (punishment for attempting to commit offences punishable with imprisonment for life or other imprisonment) of the Indian Penal Code Police said they would scan the footage of ATM kiosk’s CCTV cameras, as the miscreants couldn’t reach their DVOR.
