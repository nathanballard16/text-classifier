RTC strike leads to surge in metro passengers

Trains making 810 trips a day

The ongoing TSRTC strike is witnessing more patronage to the Hyderabad Metro Rail (HMR) service with the authorities announcing on Monday that the passenger count could touch an all time high of 3.80 lakh.

By evening, about 3.05 lakh passengers had made use of the metro transport service across the two corridors, according to HMR Managing Director N.V.S. Reddy.

Ever since the RTC workers’ unions went on strike, the metro trains have been doing 810 trips a day, 100 trips more than the usual schedules on two corridors – L.B. Nagar to Miyapur (Corridor I) and from Nagole to Hi-Tec City (Corridor 3).

The frequency of trains on the first corridor is about 3.5 minutes, whereas the frequency of trains on the third corridor is 4 minutes with the trains commencing operations from 6 a.m. in the morning, he added.
