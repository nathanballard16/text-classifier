In Jharkhand, BJP to contest 13 of 14 LS seats, ally AJSU to fight on 1 seat

File Image of BJP general secretary Bhupender Yadav | Photo Credit: ANI

New Delhi: Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) will contest 13 of the 14 Lok Sabha seats in Jharkhand while its ally partner All Jharkhand Students Union (AJSU) will fight on one seat in the upcoming Lok Sabha elections.

"In Lok Sabha elections, Bharatiya Janata Party will fight on 13 out of 14 seats and All Jharkhand Students Union (AJSU) will fight on one seat from Jharkhand,” BJP general secretary Bhupender Yadav said, according to news agency ANI.

The announcement was made following the party’s parliamentary board meeting, which was called to chalk out a strategy for the 2019 Lok Sabha elections.

Meanwhile, in the meeting, it was also decided that Prime Minister Narendra Modi will contest the general elections from Varanasi. PM Modi is MP from Varanasi constituency. However, the party has not made any official announcement.

The Lok Sabha elections are due to take place between April and May. The Election Commission is expected to announce the election dates soon.

Prime Minister Modi, BJP chief Amit Shah and other senior leaders like Sushma Swaraj, Arun Jaitley, Nitin Gadkari, Rajnath Singh among others were present in the meeting.
