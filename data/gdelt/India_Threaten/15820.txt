Uttar Pradesh Police warn rumour mongers ahead of Ayodhya verdict

Representational Image | Photo Credit: PTI

Lucknow: Just a day ahead of the Ayodhya verdict, Uttar Pradesh Police chief OP Singh on Friday warned the rumour mongers from spreading fake messages on social media about the Ayodhya dispute before and after the Supreme Court verdict which is all set to be delivered on November 9.

In a statement released by UP Police, DGP OP Singh said, "An advisory has already been sent out to people to cross-check information shared to them on the Ayodhya dispute before sharing it with others on social media. Wrong information can become a big problem for lakhs of people and result in deteriorating the law and order situation in Uttar Pradesh. And you will be solely responsible for it."

"UP Police is constantly monitoring social media platforms like WhatsApp, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, etc. So, if you feel you will go untraced for any wrong information that you will share on the internet, then it will be your misbelief," further read the statement.

OP Singh also reiterated that number 112, Twitter service and nearby police stations will be available to receive complaints on fake news spread on social media and the Uttar Pradesh will be determined to provide assist the complainants.

"If anybody or any group attempts to spread around inflammatory and offensive information then notify the police through the above-mentioned channels at the earliest," said OP Singh in the statement.

A five judge bench of the Supreme Court will deliver verdict in the Ayodhya dispute on November 9 at 10:30 am. Ahead of the verdict, section 144 has been imposed across several areas in Uttar Pradesh, Delhi and Jammu.
