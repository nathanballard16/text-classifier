New Delhi: The Defence Ministry on Thursday authorised “every member of the Coast Guard” to “visit, board, search and seize, or arrest any person” or suspicious vessels within India’s exclusive economic zone.

“In exercise of powers conferred by sub-section (3) of section 121 of the Coast Guard Act, 1978 (30 of 1978) the Central Government hereby authorize every member of the Coast Guard, constituted under section 4 of the said Act to visit, board, search and seize, or arrest any person, or seize any artificial island or any floating or moored object or any underwater object including any maritime property involved or suspected to be used or likely to be used in the commission of any offence punishable under any of the central acts including the Acts specified in clause (i) of sub-section (1) of section 121 of the said Act, within the area of maritime zone of India extending up to exclusive economic zone, as defined in clause (m) of section 2 of the said Act,” the Ministry of Defence said in a notification.

Coastal security has been a looming concern for the Indian government in wake of the continuing threat from terrorist organisations such as the Islamic State (ISIS) in the southern part of India.

Cases of terrorists hijacking boats of fishermen and trawlers to enter Indian territory along the coasts of Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal and by empowering the Coast Guards, the government has taken a step closer to enhancing coastal security.

The Indian Coast Guard time and again conducts awareness programmes to sensitise fishermen in accordance with the central government’s ‘Swachh Sagar Abhiyan’.

The fishing populace is advised to carry life-saving equipment, boat registration papers, Aadhaar cards and operate in groups to ensure they can help one another in case of an emergency.
