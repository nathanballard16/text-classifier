Dakha bypoll: Ludhiana rural cops book two for threatening voters, distributing money

LUDHIANA: Ludhiana (Rural) police on Thursday booked two villagers for distributing money for votes and tearing the posters and flex boards of Congress party candidate ahead of Dakha by-poll on October 21. The accused were identified as Gurpreet Singh Gopi and Jaswant Singh of Phallewal.In his police complaint, the complainant Satnam Singh, the sarpanch of Phallewal village, said that various parties have put up their posters and flex boards ahead of the Dakha by-election. He added that the accused, who are Akali Dal workers, along with other accomplices were giving money to locals for votes. They were also tearing the posters and flex boards of the Congress party candidate.The complainant said that one of the accused also threatened to kill those who support Congress candidate.Following a complaint from Satnam Singh, the police have booked the accused under Sections 171-C (Undue influence at elections), 294 (Obscene acts and songs), 506 (criminal intimidation), 148 (Rioting, armed with deadly weapon), 149 (unlawful assembly) and 427 (mischief causing damage to Rs 50 or more) of IPC.
