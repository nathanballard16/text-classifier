Thunderstorms likely on Friday, Saturday

KSDMA warns heavy rainfall may trigger landslips in hilly regions

The State is likely to experience strong thunderstorm activity on Friday and Saturday, the India Meteorological Department (IMD) has warned.

Thunderstorm accompanied by gusty winds with speeds reaching 40-50 kmph and lightning can be expected at isolated places in the State on both days, IMD warned.

In view of the warning, the Kerala State Disaster Management Authority (KSDMA) has issued an advisory on precautions to be taken during lightning activity. Lightning is likely to occur during summer showers between 2 p.m. and 8 p.m, according to the KSDMA.

During lightning activity, it is advisable to seek shelter inside buildings. Electrical appliances should be disconnected from power sources. Windows and doors should be kept shut. Avoid using phones, standing beneath trees or entering waterbodies, KSDMA said.

KSDMA has also warned that heavy rainfall is likely to trigger landslips in hilly regions.

Night travel

The public has been asked to avoid night travel, between 7 p.m. and 7 a.m., to the hilly areas of Malappuram, Palakkad, Wayanad, Kozhikode and Thrissur districts.

Water level in the rivers and canals are also likely to rise, the KSDMA said, urging people not to avoid entering water bodies during heavy rain.

Temperature rise

Meanwhile, the IMD has warned that maximum temperature is likely to be above the normal by two to three degrees in Thiruvananthapuram, Alappuzha and Palakkad districts on Friday.
