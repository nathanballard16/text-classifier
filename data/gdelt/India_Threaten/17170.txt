Mid-air scare: Hyderabad-bound IndiGo flight returns to Delhi airport after caution message

New Delhi: A Hyderabad-bound IndiGo A32 flight had to return to Delhi airport following take-off on Wednesday after the pilot noticed a caution advisory. The 6E-552 aircraft returned to Delhi for further inspection after the mid-air scare.

"IndiGo A320 (6E-552) operating Delhi to Hyderabad had a caution message during flight. Taking the precautionary message, pilot followed the standard operating procedure. Aircraft returned to Delhi for further inspection," the airline said in a statement.

Earlier on Tuesday, a Chennai to Coimbatore IndiGo flight, carrying 147 passengers including Congress leader and former Union finance minister P Chidambaram, suffered a technical glitch just hours before the take-off. The flight was scheduled to leave the Chennai airport at 6:15 in the morning.
