India, Pak trade fire in Poonch

POONCH, Mar 23: India and Pakistan armies Saturday traded heavy fire after the latter resorted to unprovoked firing on forward Indian posts and villages along the Line of Control (LoC) in Poonch district.

According to a defence spokesman, the small arms firing from across the border in Poonch sector started at around 5.30 PM in Kerni and Qasba sectors of Poonch district. The Indian army retaliated befittingly, the spokesman said. He said there was no report of any casualty on the Indian side in the latest ceasefire violation by Pakistan. Intermittent firing was still till the last reports poured in.

Local sources said Pakistani troops targetted Shahpur, Qasba and Kerni areas, forcing people to take shelter inside their homes.

The skirmishes between the two sides witnessed a spurt after India's preemptive air strike on a Jaish-e-Mohammed terror camp in Balakot on February 26 following the February 14 Pulwama terror attack in which 40 CRPF personnel were killed.

Five persons - four civilians and a soldier - were killed and several others injured as Pakistan army targetted dozens of villages in over 125 incidents of ceasefire violations along the LoC in the state, especially in the twin districts of Poonch and Rajouri, since then.

According to official sources, Pakistan army suffered heavy casualties and a number of its posts were destroyed in the retaliation action by the India army over the past couple of weeks.

Lt Gen Ranbir Singh, Commander Udhampur-based Northern Command, had on Friday visited forward posts in Rajouri and Reasi sectors to interact with soldiers and review the operational preparedness. He was accompanied by White Knight Corps Commander Lt Gen Paramjeet Singh.
