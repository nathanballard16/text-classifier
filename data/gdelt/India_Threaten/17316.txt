CAB Bill: Protests against Citizenship Bill continue in Guwahati with mass hunger strike

GUWAHATI: Students, senior citizens, artistes, teachers continued their protest against the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2019 in Guwahati with a mass hunger strike hours after President Ram Nath Kovind gave his assent to the bill turning it into an Act.The situation in the entire state is peaceful so far as several areas remain under curfew and Army deployment.Thousands of people have converged at Chandmari playground defying the curfew in Guwahati and sat on the hunger strike from morning convened by All Assam Students Union (AASU). The people pledged to continue with the protest and said in one voice that they would not accept the new Act.“We will never accept and allow this Act to be implemented in Assam. If this Act can be bad for certain areas where it will not be implemented, how can it be good for Assam?” AASU chief advisor Samujjal Bhattacharya said. “We have now the option of taking the battle to the court and we are soon moving the Supreme Court to challenge the validity of the new Act,” he added.The Act now will make members of Hindu, Sikh, Buddhist, Jain, Parsi and Christian communities who have come from Pakistan, Bangladesh and Afghanistan till December 31, 2014, and facing religious persecution Indian citizenship.Four northeastern states - Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur and Mizoram having the inner line permit regime (restricted travel to these areas) and areas administered under 6th schedule of Constitution in Meghalaya, Assam and Tripura are kept out of the ambit of the Act, which means that this new category of citizens will not be allowed to live or trade in these areas.Two persons were killed on Thursday evening in police firing in Guwahati. Mobile internet will be suspended till Saturday and authorities said that curfew in the city could be lifted if the situation improves.Business establishment and offices are closed but markets have been opened. Petrol depots have gone dry and are closed.Police and civil administration have engaged a large workforce to clean the roads which are littered with burnt remains of vehicles, trees, tyres and other materials.
