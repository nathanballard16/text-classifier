Nethravati river swells, residents asked to shift

MANGALURU: Nethravati river in Dakshina Kannada district is swelling due to heavy rain and increased inflow. Water in the river touched the danger level at many places on Friday.Dakshina Kannada district administration has warned people residing on the banks of the river to shift to safer places. Meanwhile, a team of officials with rescue forces have been deployed to help residents.“Residents on either side of the Nethravati river between Uppinangady and Mangaluru should shift immediately. Officials have been camping in identified spots where there are possibilities of flooding. Our teams are fully prepared to face any situation,” said deputy commissioner S Sasikanth Senthil.Water level at Nethravati river in Bantwal has gone up to 10.35 metres against the danger level of 8.5 metres. Water level of the river at Uppinangady has touched 31.5 metres, which is the danger level. Water level in Kumaradhara river at Uppinangady has increased to 26 metres against the danger level of 26.5 metres. Water level in Gundya river too touched the maximum level of five metres on Friday.The district administration has opened 30 gates of Thumbe dam in Bantwal taluk, which has a maximum storage level of 7 metres, following an increase in inflow. Similarly, 18 gates of AMR dam also have been opened after the water level increased to 18.75 metres against the maximum level of 18.9 metres.Meanwhile, stretches on national highways and state highways in the district have been flooded due to heavy rain. Stretches on Bengaluru – Mangaluru national highway at Panjala, Valalu, Udane and Lavathadka in Uppinangady have been flooded. While the flood water level on national highway at Panjala, Valalu and Udane is three feet, the road stretch at Lavathadka is under two feet water.
