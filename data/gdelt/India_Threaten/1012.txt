Weather update: North India may receive more rains and hailstorm

21 trains were running late at New Delhi station as of Wednesday morning. (Image- Getty)

The northern region of the country looks set for yet another day of rain and snowfall as India Meteorological Department (IMD) predicts that the Western Disturbance will continue to reign.

Temperatures in the early morning will be much warmer than normal over Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar and adjoining states. However, maximum temperatures will be cooler than normal.

IMD has said that Punjab, Haryana, Chandigarh and Delhi along with Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh will receive light to moderate rain over next 24 hours. It also predicted hailstorm is likely in Madhya Pradesh.

The himalayan states of Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand experienced snowfall on Tuesday. The spell is likely to continue.

The IMD has further warned that dense to very dense fog would persist over regions in Punjab, Haryana, north Rajasthan and pockets of Delhi on Wednesday.

Air quality will remain 'poor' to 'very poor' across major cities in India.

The fog and persistent showers have affected train and flight services in North India. In Delhi, 21 trains are running late due to fog and low visibility.

Air traffic is affected due to the fog as well.
