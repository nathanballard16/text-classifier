Article 370 scrapped in Jammu and Kashmir: After Jammu, Kashmir, and Ladkah became Union Territories, these 106 changes implemented in Jammu, Kashmir, Ladakh

Article 370 scrapped in Jammu and Kashmir: It was a historic day for India and people of Jammu and Kashmir as the Narendra Modi government revoked Article 370 and 35 A. The special status to Jammu and Kashmir was also scrapped. Reading the bill in Parliamemt today Union Home Minister Amit Shah announced that Jammu and Kashmir will not be a state anymore and awarded the status of a Union Territory (UT) with legislative assemmbly. He added that Ladakh will be a saperated Union Territory with out assembly like Chandigarh.

The bill was supported by 125 Rajya Sabha members and 61 MPs voted against.

Former Chief Ministers Mehbooba Mufti and Omar Abdullah, who were placed under house arrest Yesterday, were taken into custody today.

Now Indian Penal Code will come into force instead of Ranbir Penal Code (RPC).

Key highlights from today’s event:

-No more speacial status to Jammu and Kashmir.

-Abolishment of Article 370 and Article 35A.

-Home Minister Amit Shah announced that Jammu and Kashmir will be a union territory with a legislature.

-Ladakh Has been separated from Jammua and Kashmir and will now be a separate union territory without a legislature.

-People from rest of India now can buy property in Jammu, Kashmir, Ladakh.

-Earlier, only permanent residents were allowed to apply for the state government jobs and addmission into educational institutions, now any one can take apply the same.

-Indian flag will be the only flag allowed in the state. Earlier, state had its own flag.

-India will now have control for defence, external affairs, and communication.
