Northeast in grip of cold wave, Imphal shivers at 1.6°C

GUWAHATI: The northeast is in the grip of a cold wave with the Mercury dipping below 10 degrees Celsius in many parts of the region. Imphal has recorded a minimum temperature of 1.6 degrees Celsius.The Met department, in its bulletin on Monday, said shallow to moderate fog is very likely to occur at isolated pockets over East Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Meghalaya, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram and Tripura.Thunderstorm with lightning is very likely to occur at isolated places over South Assam and Manipur.It has warned that dense to very dense fog is very likely to occur at isolated pockets over Assam, Meghalaya, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram and Tripura in the next 24 hours. The forecast says snowfall is very likely to occur at a few places over Arunachal Pradesh and light to moderate rain is very likely to occur at a few places over Assam, Meghalaya, Manipur, Mizoram and Tripura. Light rain is very likely to occur at isolated places over Nagaland on January 3.A senior official of the IMD said considering the prevailing weather conditions in the northeast, the minimum temperature may decline further in the next couple of days.“Due to passing western disturbance as an upper air trough, effecting the NE region, the NE wtnessed rainfall and snowfall in the last 24 hours. This has resulted in adip in the minimum temperature,” said the official.
