Goa Congress plans to protest, show black billboards at IFFI 2019 opening ceremony

Goa Congress plans to protest at IFFI 2019

Key Highlights The 50th edition of International Film Festival of India is all set to begin today The Congress plans to stage a protest with black billboards on its opening ceremony The protest will be against a local marine coming up in the state

The much-awaited International Film Festival of India (IFFI) is all set to kick-start today. The Congress, however, plans to show black billboards and stage a protest at the film festival.

The local Goa Congress plans to protest amidst foreign delegates and Union Minister Prakash Javadekar against a local marina coming up in the state, as per Times Now reports.

Prohibitory orders have been passed to ensure that there's no law and order situation in the city.

The 50th edition of IFFI has many highlights to attract ardent cinema lovers. The inauguration ceremony, which is to be hosted today, will see megastars Amitabh Bachchan and Rajinikanth in attendance, amongst others. The opening ceremony will be hosted by Bollywood filmmaker-producer Karan Johar, who will compere the ceremony in the presence of Union Minister for Information and Broadcasting Prakash Javadekar, Secretary for Information and Broadcasting Amit Khare, and Goa Chief Minister Pramod Sawant among other delegates.

As for IFFI, this edition marks the 50th anniversary of the film festival. This year, over 9000 delegates have signed for the event and the film line up includes over 200 films, out of which 26 feature films and 15 non-feature films are part of the Indian Panorama section. At the festival, India is set to showcase the power of cinema and people from all across the country will gather at the film festival.
