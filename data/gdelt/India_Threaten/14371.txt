Modi to address 4 rallies in Haryana

Chandigarh: With the process of filing of the nominations over, BJP has released the list of 40 star campaigners for intensified campaigning. Prime Minister Narendra Modi will address one political rally each in southern Haryana, on GT Road, in Rohtak area and in Ahirwal/Mewat.

Union home minister Amit Shah, defence minister Rajnath Singh and BJP working president J P Nadda will address a total 24 election rallies. The dates for these events are to be finalized. Voting will be held in Haryana on October 21.

Meanwhile, chief ministers of BJP-ruled states as well as the Union cabinet ministers and national-level leaders of the party will be camping in all districts, in addition to their duties as star campaigners. All the 10 MPs of Haryana, Union minister Smriti Irani, Delhi MP Hans Raj Hans, Punjab MP Sunny Deol and other senior leaders they will be addressing the rallies in accordance to the demography of the constituency.

“Our teams are working on their schedule. Leaders will start coming from Monday. Despite the fact that BJP is in strong position due to performance of the state as well as the Centre, the party does not want to miss any chance now. Elections are elections,” said Randeep Ghanghas, BJP spokesperson.

Besides cabinet ministers and MPs, CM Manohar Lal Khattar, BJP state president Subhash Barala, Yogeshwar Dutt, Sandeep Singh, Babita Phogat and Sonali Phogat will also be campaigning for candidates of their nearby constituencies, along with fighting their own elections.
