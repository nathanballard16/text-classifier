Summer heat: KSDMA advises extreme caution

The Kerala State Disaster Management Authority (KSDMA) has advised extreme caution as day temperatures are likely to remain high in the State till Sunday.

The KSDMA has advised the public to avoid direct sunlight between 11 a.m. and 3 p.m. on these days as chances of sunstroke/sunburn are high.

Citing India Meteorological Department’s forecasts, the KSDMA has warned that day temperatures are likely to be above the normal by two to four degrees in Thiruvananthapuram, Kollam, Alappuzha and Palakkad till Saturday.

The warning is also valid for Kottayam, Pathanamthitta, Idukki, Ernakulam, Thrissur, Malappuram, Kozhikode, Kannur, and Kasaragod. Travellers have been advised to carry water in bottles to avoid dehydration.

Advisory

Children, pregnant women and the elderly, and people suffering from ailments have been asked to avoid outdoor activities between 11 a.m. and 3 p.m.

The public have been asked to drink lots of water and, ideally, avoid tea, coffee and alcohol during the daytime.

Employers have been instructed to strictly follow the revised daytime working hours prescribed by the Labour Commissioner. Politicians engaged in Lok Sabha election work, the police and delivery staff of online food suppliers have been advised to take adequate precautions.
