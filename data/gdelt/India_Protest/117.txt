Indian women use cover of night to beat ancient temple ban

Two Indian women became the first to enter a famous Hindu temple yesterday after a centuries-old ban was overturned.

Months of mass protests had prevented women from gaining access to the Sabarimala temple in Kerala, one of the most holy sites for Hindus. This was despite a declaration three months ago by the supreme court in Delhi, which stated that the temple’s historic ban on women “of menstruating age”, defined as between ten and 50 years old, was illegal.
