TS doctors protest against NMC Bill in Delhi

Medical students associations and Indian Medical Association (IMA) leaders from Telangana have participated in the protest against National Medical Commission (NMC) Bill staged in Delhi on Monday morning. The representatives said that their protest will continue in Hyderabad from Tuesday.

Associations comprising junior and senior doctors have been protesting against the bill claiming it to be anti-poor, anti-people. They demanded that the bill be withdrawn from ongoing Parliament sessions.

IMA-Telangana president Dr B Pratap Reddy, honorary general secretary Sanjeev Singh Yadav, Healthcare Reforms Doctors Association president Dr K Mahesh Kumar, Nizam’s Institute of Medical Sciences (NIMS) Resident Doctors Association (RDA) president Dr G Srinivas, Telangana Junior Doctors Association (TJUDA) members participated in the protests on Monday.

Dr Mahesh Kumar said that apart from Section 32 of the NMC Bill which allows limited licence, they are against other sections of the bill. According to Section 32, “the Commission may grant limited licence to practice medicine at mid-level as Community Health Provider to such person connected with modern scientific medical profession who qualify such criteria as may be specified by the regulations”.
