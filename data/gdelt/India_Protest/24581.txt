PM Modi congratulates Hemant Soren, Raghubar Das finds no mention

RANCHI: After BJP's poor show in the state assembly election, Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Monday evening took to Twitter and congratulated Hemant Soren and the Mahagathbandhan-led alliance for its victory. The PM had held 10 rallies in the run up to the election to garner support for his party's candidates."Congratulations to @HemantSoren Ji and the JMM-led alliance for the victory in the Jharkhand polls. Best wishes to them in serving the state," Modi's tweet read.In another tweet, he patted BJP for its efforts in the state. "I thank the people of Jharkhand for having given @BJP4India the opportunity to serve the state for many years. I also applaud the hardworking Party Karyakartas for their efforts. We will continue serving the state and raising people-centric issues in the times to come," Modi said. However, Raghubar Das did not find any mention in the PM's tweet.As the counting trends showed the JMM-led alliance heading towards a victory, West Bengal chief minister Mamata Banerjee congratulated Hemant in the evening. "People of Jharkhand have entrusted U to fulfill their aspirations. My good wishes to all brothers/sisters in Jharkhand. Elections were held during #CAA_NRC_Protest. This is a verdict in favour of citizens," Mamata wrote. Thanking her for her wishes, Hemant replied, "This has been a battle to establish democratic will & socially inclusive Jharkhand. I dedicate this victory to the people of Jharkhand."After Mamata's tweet, congratulatory messages kept coming think and fast. Among those who congratulated Hemant are Delhi chief minister Arvind Kejriwal, Punjab chief minister Capt Amarinder Singh, former UP chief minister Akhilesh Yadav and Upendra Khuswaha among others.
