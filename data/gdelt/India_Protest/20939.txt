The Amritsar disclosures

A profound impression has been made in this country by the arrival of Indian newspapers containing reports of the evidence given before the Committee, presided over by Lord Hunter, which is inquiring into the disorders in India last April. It was vaguely known that severe measures were taken at Amritsar, the religious capital of the Sikhs and the scene of the worst outbreaks. Our Correspondent at Simla telegraphed at the time that at Amritsar “the troops dispersed the rioters on Sunday with very heavy casualties”. It was certainly not known that an unarmed gathering which had met, in defiance of a military proclamation, in an enclosed space at Amritsar, had been fired upon without warning, and that 400 had been killed.
