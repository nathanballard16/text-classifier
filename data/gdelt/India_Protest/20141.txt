CAB protest: Assam continues to remain on edge; PM Modi assures to safeguard people's rights

GUWAHATI: Police have resorted to firing in the air at several areas in Guwahati to disperse hundreds of protestors as Assam continues to remain on the edge on Thursday, a day after the contentious Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2019 was passed by Rajya Sabha.After a violent protest in Guwahati on Wednesday, which continued till late night, Army was deployed, curfew was launched and mobile internet was suspended.But all measures by the government have proved futile as hundreds of people in Guwahati have defied the indefinite curfew and are coming out of their home on the street to protest against the new law that will make lakh of illegal Hindu migrants from Bangladesh till December 2014 citizens of India.Union home ministry has released an IPS of Assam cadre GP Singh, who was posted IGP in NIA on deputation and sent him back to Guwahati. The officer, who is known as anti-terrorism expert during his tenure here, arrived this morning, is likely to be posted as ADGP, law and order.Prime minister Narendra Modi tweeted, “I want to assure my brothers and sisters of Assam that they have nothing to worry after the passing of #CAB. I want to assure them- no one can take away your rights, unique identity and beautiful culture. It will continue to flourish and grow.”Protests are also taking place in Nagoan, Jorhat and Dibrugarh
