Top five news stories to watch out for on May 2, 2019

A Bench led by Chief Justice Ranjan Gogoi will hear today the Election Commission’s response on why it delayed action regarding the Congress’ complaints against Prime Minister Narendra Modi and BJP party president Amit Shah. The Congress has alleged that Modi and Shah have given hate speeches and used the armed forces for political propaganda.

The BJP has announced a State-wide bandh in Telangana today over the alleged lapses by the Telangana State Board of Intermediate Education. More than 20 students in the State committed suicide as they failed to clear the exams. The Telangana government has constituted a three-member committee to probe the allegations.

Congress chief Rahul Gandhi will address a public meeting in Rajasthan’s Sikar district today, in support of the party’s candidate Subhash Maharia. He is also scheduled to hold a rally in Simdega, under the Khunti parliamentary constituency of Jharkhand, today.

The US’ waiver on Iran sanctions will end today. This means that India has to stop importing crude oil from Iran or face heavy sanctions from the US. This is likely to push up fuel prices in India.

Online registration for Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions 2019 will open today. This year, the single-window counselling for admission to BE/B.Tech courses will be conducted by the Directorate of Technical Education. Last date for filing the application is May 31.
