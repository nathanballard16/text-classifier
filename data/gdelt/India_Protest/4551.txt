Top five news stories to watch out for March 9 and March 10

BJP president Amit Shah is will attend a key meet of the RSS's Akhil Bharatiya Pratinidhi Sabha (ABPS) on March 9. RSS chief Bhagwat is scheduled to address the concluding session of the ABPS on March 10. The three-day long annual meeting begun in Gwalior on Friday.

Oil Minister Dharmendra Pradhan will meet his Saudi Arabian counterpart Khalid al-Falih on Saturday. Saudi Arabia is India's biggest crude oil supplier after Iraq. The Saudi national oil company Saudi Aramco, along with the UAE's ADNOC and state-owned Indian partners have committed to invest $44 billion to build a 1.2 million barrels per day (bpd) refinery and petrochemical project in western India.

The Aam Aadmi Party will launch a movement demanding for full statehood for New Delhi from March 10. During the launch, a letter from the Delhi Chief Minister and AAP party chief Arvind Kejriwal will be presented to the citizens of the nation's capital. Watch out for a massive rally with 1,000 teams from the AAP protest across the city.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi will review a CISF parade and address troops on March 10 as the paramilitary force marks the 50th year of its raising. The event will be held at the Central Industrial Security Forces' 5th battalion camp in Indirapuram in Ghaziabad on March 10.

The Congress and the Janata Dal (Secular) are likely to announce their seat-sharing formula for the Lok Sabha elections on Sunday. The discussions for the same started on Thursday when JD(S) leader and former Prime Minister HD Deve Gowda met Congress President Rahul Gandhi.
