Naveen Patnaik: Amid protests over demolition, Naveen Patnaik announces redevelopment package for Ekamra Kshetra

BHUBANESWAR: Amid stiff protests over the demolition drive around the Lingaraj Temple, Odisha chief minister Naveen Patnaik on Thursday announced a 66-acre Ekamra Kshetra (old town) redevelopment plan for the 11th century shrine and its periphery area.Total nine projects including preservation of heritage sites, development of parking space and preservation of heritage water bodies are part of the redevelopment plan. Around Rs 700 crore would be spent for the redevelopment plan, said an official after Naveen reviewed the development here at a high-level meeting.“Besides clearing the encroachments, process for which is on, the state government also requires some private land for the redevelopment plan,” the official said.As his appeal to people in Puri to sacrifice their land helped the administration to acquire private land around Jagannath Temple for development of security zone, Naveen made similar appeal to people in the city. He also announced a rehabilitation package for the affected residents and vendors.The redevelopment plan includes a magnificent entrance, restoration of Bindu Sagar lake, development of four parking lots, Lingaraj heritage project and development of Kedar Gouri Temple and Mukteswar Temple. A heritage interpretation centre and e-auto programme are also part of the project.“All the structures in front of the main entrance of the temple will be removed for free passage of visitors. The front areas of the shrine will be used as pedestrian area while movement of vehicles will be restricted. The Lingaraj police station and temple trust office will be shifted to nearby places by demolishing the structure,” said a works department official after Naveen’s announcement.Construction of road from Taleswar Square to Badhei Banka square, development of road around the shrine, beautification and development of greenery of areas around the temple, facilities for smooth darshan are also part the redevelopment package Besides development of Bindu Sagar lake, steps will also be taken for preservation of other heritage water bodies like Kedar Gouri, Papanasini, Godavari, Kotitirtha, Ganga Yamuna and Devipapahara. A decision has also been taken to step up the security for the temple.With many people are expected to lose their livelihood because of the ongoing demolition drive near the shrine, the chief minister announced rehabilitation package for the affected.The road side vendors will get one-time assistance of Rs 30,000 in two installments while they will also be rehabilitated in vending zones. The shopkeepers, whose structures have been razed by the administration, will get monthly assistance of Rs 10,000 for 12 months. Subsequently, they will be accommodated in the market complex to be developed by Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation and state tourism department.The chief minister said the affected residents and commercial establishments near the temple will get adequate compensation after negotiation.
