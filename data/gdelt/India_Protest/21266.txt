India protests erupt over controversial citizenship law

Protests in New Delhi against the new 'anti-Muslim' citizenship law erupted on 15 December when police used tear gas to disperse demonstrators at a major university, injuring more than two dozen activists.

People took to the streets since 9 December when the government of India’s Prime Minister Narendra Modi, as part of its nationalist program, offered a controversial bill in parliament, that allows citizenship for Hindus, Sikhs, Buddhists, Jains, Parsis, and Christians who illegally migrated...
