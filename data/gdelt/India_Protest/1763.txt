Burglary takes place in Punjab National Bank in Trichy; gold, cash worth crores looted from 5 lockers

Robbery in PNB,Trichy | Photo Credit: PTI

In a case of a burglary reported from a Punjab National Bank's branch located in Trichy district over the weekend, robbers were successful in stealing contents of five lockers amounting to crores of rupees. The branch, located at Samayapuram near the Chennai-Trichy highway, was closed over the weekend. The employees on Monday discovered the theft when they returned from the holiday.

According to the police, the burglars entered into the building by breaking a hole on one of the walls. The bank reportedly shares a wall with a private school. The burglars used a gas welding machine to dig out the hole in the wall and enter the premises.

Around 2,000 people have an account in the branch and nearly four kgs of gold and, cash was kept in the lockers. Only the branch manager and the assistant manager have the keys to the strongroom.

While the burglars disabled the alarm system with the help of a wire cutter, reportedly 500 sovereigns of gold along with cash have been stolen. Samayapuram toll gate police station has registered a case and the investigation is on. Meanwhile, it is also being said that an inside employee might have had a hand in the robbery.

After this, another incident of a robbery was reported in a departmental store located 2 km away from the bank. Trichy police have been searching the CCTV footage of the area.
