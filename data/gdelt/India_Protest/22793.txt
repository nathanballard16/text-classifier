Three dead as protests grow against India’s ‘anti-Muslim’ law

Thousands were arrested and at least three people killed as protests broke out across India against “anti-Muslim” legislation, in a big challenge to the country’s Hindu nationalist government.

Mobile phone and internet services were cut for several hours in some cities as the authorities tried to quell disturbances amid chaotic scenes. It raised questions about the government’s ability to deal with the growing demonstrations. Some states said that they would ignore a federal order to help to compile a register of citizens.
