New Delhi protests against Pakistan SC ruling on PoK, calls it 'interference in 'India's internal matters'

File photo (PoK) | Photo Credit: ANI

New Delhi: India, on Friday reiterated that Pakistan has íllegally' occupied parts of Jammu and Kashmir (J&K) and lodged a protest against its Supreme Court's order on Gilgit Baltistan region and said that it is an 'interference in India's internal matters'. The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), during a press conference, said that the Deputy High Commissioner of Pakistan was summoned over the Pakistan Sc order.

"The Deputy High Commissioner of Pakistan was summoned today and a strong protest was lodged on a recent order by Supreme Court of Pakistan on the so-called "Gilgit-Baltistan” which is an interference in India’s internal affairs," MEA said.

"It was reiterated that entire J&K, which also includes so-called 'Gilgit-Baltistan' has been, is and shall remain an integral part of India. Pakistan Government or judiciary have no locus-standi on territories illegally and forcibly occupied by it," it further said. On Thursday, a seven-judge bench of the Pakistan Supreme Court extended the powers of the top court to the Gilgit-Baltistan region and asked the Pakistan government to give rights to people of the region.

“India and Pakistan are responsible for giving more rights to the people of the areas under their control and until the referendum happens, Pakistan is bound to give Gilgit-Baltistan as many rights as possible,” the court ruling was quoted by Daily Times Pakistan.
