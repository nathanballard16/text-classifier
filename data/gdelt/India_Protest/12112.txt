Hundreds held as protesters in Jammu and Kashmir defy Modi lockdown

Protesters in Kashmir have defied an extensive security crackdown to vent their fury at India’s move to impose direct rule, prompting deadly clashes despite a stifling curfew.

With local politicians arrested and a total blackout of phone and internet services extending into a third day, demonstrations were reported in Srinagar and across the territory. A total of 560 people were detained in the cities of Srinagar, Baramulla and Gurez, according to the Indian Express.
