Pakistan violates ceasefire in J&K's Poonch, security forces retaliating: Reports

Representational image I Photo credit: PTI | Photo Credit: PTI

Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir: Pakistan has violated ceasefire in Mendhar sector of Poonch, security forces are retaliating accordingly, reported the news agency ANI.

The incident comes just a week after India summoned a Pakistan High Commission official and lodged a strong protest over repeated ceasefire violations from across the border leading to the loss of many lives on the Indian side.

The Ministry of External Affairs in a strongly worded protest on December 7, said that despite repeated calls for restraint and requests to adhere to the ceasefire understanding, Pakistani forces conducted over 1,962 instances of unprovoked violations in which 50 Indians lost their lives.

The MEA had also urged Islamabad to keep its side of the bargain in the bilateral commitment of not allowing its soil to be used by terrorists to attack India in any way.

Apart from this, New Delhi also lodged a protest over the rise in the number of terrorist infiltration across the Line of (LoC) in Jammu and Kashmir and said that Islamabad was backing terrorists who are targeting Indian civilians and security forces.
