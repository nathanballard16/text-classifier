Punjab: 5 members of a robbers-gang nabbed in Samana

PATIALA: Ten days after some accused robbed off Rs 7.27 lakh from OBC Bank in Samana town of Patiala district , the police have nabbed five members of the robbers-gang allegedly involved in the crime. The accused were arrested on a tip-off during police checking at a barrier near Lalochi village of the district. The police recovered illegal weapons and ammunition from them.The police claimed that with the arrest of these accused four more cases of snatching and robbery that took place in Sangrur district have been solved. The members of this gang had also snatched a car in Sangrur in the recent past besides committing robberies at two different petrol pumps and snatching of money from a car occupant in the last two months.Among the arrested five accused two are from Sangrur district while three hail from Uttar Pradesh but were living on a rented house in Ludhiana. The police claimed that modus operandi of the accused was to loot at gunpoint. For this three accused used to commit the crime while others remain seated in the car to escape immediately. The police said that the motive of the accused was to rob off the money to live a luxurious life whereas the mastermind behind the bank robbery was a nineteen year old Daljit Singh who was just class seven qualified.Patiala Senior Superintendent of Police, Mandeep Singh Sidhu , said, “We have given a message to the criminals by arresting them within a stipulated time period. Our two cops have been promoted while a cash reward of Rs one lakh has been announced by the DGP Punjab. The accused are identified as Daljit Singh, 19 and Jagjit Singh Sonu, 25 of Sangrur; Prince Rao, 22, Om Prakash, 22 and Deepak Kumar, 29, all living on a rented house in Ludhiana.The police said that three more accused involved in the robbery cases have been identified as Biraj, Ashok, and Hemant of Azamgarh in UP but were out of police reach and will be arrested soon.
