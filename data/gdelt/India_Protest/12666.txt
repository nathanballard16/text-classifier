Pak summons envoy over LoC 'truce violations'

ISLAMABAD/NEW DELHI: Pakistan on Friday lodged strong protest with India, the third in as many days, over alleged ceasefire violations by troops along the Line of Control . This came even as Army sources said Pakistan had increased "ceasefire violations" in the past few days to project the front as a major flashpoint.Pakistan's foreign office summoned Indian deputy high commissioner Gaurav Ahluwalia and condemned the unprovoked firing in Lipa and Battal sectors in which it said a security person and two civilians were killed.Army sources said it was Pakistan that had increased ceasefire violations. "Pakistan has upscaled ceasefire violations both in terms of weapon and area in the last few days. Its aim appears to show Kashmir as a potential flashpoint to seek world attention," an Army source was quoted by ANI as saying.
