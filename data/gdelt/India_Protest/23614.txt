Delhi Waqf Board to give Rs 5 5 lakh each to kin of those killed during anti CAA protests

New Delhi: The Delhi Waqf Board on Saturday announced that it will give a financial assistance of Rs 5.5 lakh each to the families of those killed during violent protests against the Citizenship (Amendment) Act. Board chairman Amanatullah Khan, also an AAP MLA, in a Facebook post claimed that several people were killed in Uttar Pradesh and Karnataka's Mangalore during protests against the CAA and NRC, due to "police bullets". Khan told PTI that allegedly 20 people have died in violent protests across the country. The death toll in violent protests in UP increased to 11 on Saturday, officials said. An eight-year-old boy was killed in a stampede in Varanasi when a violent mob was being chased by policemen.
