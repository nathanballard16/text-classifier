Maharashtra CM Thackeray stays Aarey car shed project

MUMBAI: On the first day in office as the chief minister of Maharahstra, Uddhav Thackeray announced stay on construction of metro car shed project at the Aarey Colony , where protests were held last month against cutting of trees for the work.Uddhav said that Mumbai has a special place for him and he will not allow this mass-murder of trees to be done in the name of development. "I am not against development, but this mass murder will not be tolerated. I have stayed the metro car shed project at Aarey and have asked for a review. The stay is on the construction of car shed and not on metro project, but options for the car shed needs to be reviewed," said Uddhav during his first press interaction after taking charge as the CM. Shiv Sena has been protesting against the cutting of trees by the BJP government at Aarey.A Supreme Court bench had last month sought a status report with pictures on plantation, transplantation and felling of trees in Aarey colony area.The Bombay HC had on October 4 refused to declare Aarey Colony a forest and declined to quash the Mumbai municipal corporation's decision to allow felling of over 2,600 trees in the green zone to set up a metro car shed.Soon after the decision, former Maharashtra CM Devendra Fadnavis saying that it will discourage investors and stall infrastructure projects in the city.
