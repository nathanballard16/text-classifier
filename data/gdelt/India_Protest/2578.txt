KVP writes open letter to Chandrababu Naidu over Deeksha

Hyderabad: Congress senior leader and Rajya Sabha MP KVP Ramachandra Rao has written an open letter to TDP chief N Chandrababu Naidu over his Deeksha on special status in New Delhi on 11 Feb.

Through the open letter, KVP criticised Naidu that in the name of dharna and deeksha he is playing dramas and he also said that Chandrababu has no right to hold deeksha of the special status.

He alleged that, when Congress party was holding a protest for special status in Guntur they were been attacked by the TDP activists by throwing eggs and tomatoes at them, that is the history of Naidu, KVP said.

As a elections stunt, Chandrababu Naidu has worn a black shirt to mark a protest against NDA government. He is acting as if he is dethroning Modi from the central. In the name of the special status have you not changed the colors like Chameleon, KVP questioned Naidu.

After the TDP and Congress alliance in Telangana elections, KVP Ramachandra Rao was silent from the active politics.

KVP who was one of the close associates of late CM YS Rajasekhara Reddy, from the beginning they were differences between KVP and Chandrababu Naidu.

In the latest development, Congress and TDP have joined hands together for the coming elections in AP. On this note, no Congress leader is making any political allegation or comments against TDP, Chandrababu Naidu.

At this time the KVP's open letter to Chandrababu has created a confusion amount the Congress leaders in the state.
