Entire Opposition, including Cong, to boycott joint session of Parliament on Tuesday

New Delhi, Nov 25 (UNI) Congress and other opposition parties on Monday decided to boycott the Constitution day celebration in the Central Hall of Parliament on Tuesday, in protest against the 'violation of the Constitutional norms' in Maharashtra and other parties in the country.

On the occasion of the Constitution Day, a joint session of Parliament will be addressed by President Ram Nath Kovind and Prime Minister Narendra Modi among others in the Central hall of Parliament.

Congress will also hold protest from 1000 hurs to 1100 hrs on Tuesday near the Ambedkar statue in Parliament.

It is learnt that constituents of the UPA, including the NCP, DMK, RJD, and other opposition parties like the Shiv Sena, the Samajwadi party and the All India Trinamool Congress, also plan to boycott the function being organised to celebrate 70 years of the Constitution.

On Monday Congress and other opposition parties protested inside and outside Parliament against the government formation in Maharashtra by BJP's Devendra Fadnavis and called it a 'murder of democracy'.
