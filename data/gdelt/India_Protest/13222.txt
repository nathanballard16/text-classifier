NEW DELHI: Benchmark equity indices BSE Sensex and NSE Nifty opened higher on Tuesday, mirroring firm cues from global markets amid ease in US-China trade war fears.US President Donald Trump on Monday flagged the possibility of a trade deal with China and said he believes Beijing was sincere in its desire to reach an agreement, Reuters reported. MSCI 's broadest index of Asia-Pacific shares outside Japan was up 0.2 per cent after dropping 1.3 per cent the previous day. South Korea's KOSPI added 0.8 per cent and Japan's Nikkei rose 1 per cent.Meanwhile, investor sentiment also got a boost following the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) decision to transfer a record Rs 1.76 lakh crore dividend and surplus reserves to the government. There are reports that the reserve would either be used to provide fiscal stimulus to a sagging economy or reduce off-balance sheet borrowings. It may also be used to meet the expected shortfall in revenue collections.Domestic currency too rallied against the US dollar after settling below the 72-mark for the first time in nine months on Monday. The rupee opened 34 paise higher at 71.68 against the greenback.The 30-share Sensex opened 164 points higher at 37,658, while its NSE counterpart Nifty rose 48.70 points to 11,107.As many as 25 stocks in the Sensex pack were trading in the green in early trade with Tata Steel gaining the most with 2.77 per cent. It was followed by Tata Motors (up 2.76 per cent), State Bank of India (up 2.34 per cent),(up 2.04 per cent) and YES Bank (up 1.75 per cent).On the other hand,, TCS and Asian Paints declined up to 1.64 per cent in early trade.Barring BSE IT (down 1.17 per cent) and Teck (down 0.95 per cent), other sectoral indices were trading on a firm note. The Capital Goods, Metal, Auto and Power indices were up over 1 per cent each.
