Civilian injured in Pak firing in Rajouri

RAJOURI, Aug 1: A civilian got injured as Pakistan violated ceasefire on the fifth consecutive day by firing unprovoked along the Line of Control (LoC) in Nowshera sector of Rajouri district in Jammu on Thursday.

“Pakistan violated the ceasefire last night in Nowshera sector of Rajouri in which a civilian suffered injuries,” official sources said.

The civilian identified as Mohammad Altaf (37), resident of Pukherni, sustained a bullet injury in neck and he was hospitalised.

Earlier on July 30, an army jawan attained martyrdom in ceasefire violation along the Line of Control in Rajouri district.

On July 29, three civilian suffered injuries in Shahpur area of Poonch including a 10-day old child, who succumbed during the treatment.

On Wednesday, India issued a strong demarche to the Pakistan High Commission against the killing of an innocent Indian civilian in an unprovoked act of firing by Pakistan forces. The firing resulted into killing of an infant boy and injuring of two civilians in the Poonch sector of Jammu and Kashmir.

New Delhi also called upon Islamabad to investigate into the incident. External Affairs Ministry shot off a letter to Pakistan High Commission in India and condemned the deliberate targeting of innocent civilians by Pakistan forces. The letter said, this act is against all established humanitarian norms and professional military conduct.
