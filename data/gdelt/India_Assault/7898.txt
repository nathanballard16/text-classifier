Custodial death puts Surendranagar police in the dock

RAJKOT: Custodial death of a fraud accused inside Surendranagar B division police station lockup has sent shock waves across the rank and file of Surendranagar police on Thursday afternoon.While the police maintain that the deceased, Kashyap Rawal (38), died of illness, family members allege police brutality killed their kin.According to police, an application of complaint was lodged against Rawal, a car dealer and resident of Neminath Society on 80-Feet Road in Surendranagar, for allegedly not paying the amount for the car he had purchased from an Ahmedabad-based car dealer. “When we called Rawal for questioning, he complained of chest pain and we rushed him to Gandhi Hospital in Surendranagar. There he died shortly after admission,” said an official from Surendranagar B division police station.However, father Himanshu Rawal has lodged a complaint against the officials of Surendranagar B division police station, accusing them of custodial torture that resulted in his son’s death. Rawal’s family members made a representation before the Surendranagar district collector, demanding an inquiry against the erring cops.Surendranagar deputy superintendent of police A B Valand said, that an inquiry was initiated into the alleged custodial death. “We approached a sub-divisional magistrate who filed an inquest on Rawal’s family members’ behalf. Then doctors of Rajkot forensic science laboratory panel conducted Rawal’s post-mortem which was videorecorded, and the report of which is being awaited,” he said.Meanwhile, the family members have denied accepting the body till the accused are booked. Rawal is survived by his wife and a son.
