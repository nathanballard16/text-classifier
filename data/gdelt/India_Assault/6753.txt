3,120 sexual assault evidence collection kits to be distributed countrywide

NEW DELHI: The Bureau of Police Research and Development (BPR&D) has procured 3,120 Sexual Assault Evidence Collection (SAEC) Kits for distribution to states and union territories. Besides this 2,575 officers have been trained by BPR&D and National Insititute of Criminology and Forensic Sciences in collection, handling and transportation of forensic evidence, including trace elements like a single strand of hair, dead skin cells, blood.The BPR&D has also procured 3,120 SAEC kits for distribution to states and UTs as orientation kit as part of training. The handling of evidence is based on the notified guidelines for collection of forensic evidence in sexual assault cases and standard composition in a sexual assault forensic kit, sources said.These measures are being cited to be part of the measures being taken by the women safety division of the ministry of home affairs to expedite investigations in a time-bound manner.According to the latest updates available with MHA, Madhya Pradesh is showing the way especially in particularly heinous cases securing the death sentence in 21 rape/POCSO Act cases since the Criminal Law (Amendment) Act came into force in 2018.As per the investigation tracking system for sexual offences (ITSSO) launched on February 19, the MHA cites Haryana, Delhi, Madhya Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh as the leading states/UTs in terms of completion of investigations within two months.MHA set up ITSSO, an online mechanism to monitor and track time-bound investigation in sexual assault cases in accordance with the 2018 amendment to the law. It is an analytical tool based on CCTNS data available right from the police station to national level.Meanwhile, the latest update on the ‘Emergency Response Support System (ERSS)’ with a single emergency number (112) shows that it has been operationalised in 20 states and UTs. These include Andaman Islands, Andhra Pradesh, Chhattisgarh , Dadra & Nagar Haveli, Daman & Diu, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Kerala, Lakshadweep, Nagaland , Puducherry, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu Telangana , Uttarakhand & Uttar Pradesh, and Mumbai city. From the Nirbhaya Fund, Rs 292.89 crore has been utilised so far for this project.On February 19, home minister Rajnath Singh made a strong pitch for time-bound investigations in cases in rape and sexual assault as mandated by the Criminal Law (Amendment) Act 2018. He was speaking at the launch of the ERSS linked to a common emergency number 112 in 16 states, Union Territories and Mumbai city. ERSS was launched in Himachal Pradesh and Nagaland late last year. Home secretary Rajiv Gauba reiterated the need to implement time-bound investigations and cited the example of Madhya Pradesh where trials have been completed anywhere between 3 to 15 days.At the launch, women and child development minister Maneka Gandhi had sought the intervention of the MHA to expedite the process of assigning rape investigation kits in all police stations.
