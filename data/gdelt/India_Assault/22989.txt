FIR against 1,000 for AMU violence during anti-CAA protests

Aligarh cops release new video showing students hurling tear gas shells

AGRA: The Rapid Action Force has filed a complaint against 1,000 unnamed protesters for violence at Aligarh Muslim University during the anti-CAA stir on December 15, a senior officer said.The force also admitted using rubber bullets, stun grenades, teargas and chilli shells to control the mob, reports Anuja Jaiswal. According to the RAF, 11 force personnel, including a commandant, were injured in the clash.The FIR has been registered under IPC Sections 147 (rioting), 153 (provocation with intent to cause riots), 332 (voluntarily causing hurt to deter public servant from his duty), 353 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty), 336 (endangering life or personal safety of others), 188 (disobedience to order duly promulgated by public servant), etc.The FIR states that two RAF companies were demanded by the district authorities on the night of December 15. “We first used water cannon but when the situation did not improve, teargas shells were used along with low-intensity lathicharge to control the mob,” read the FIR.It also states that the students “unlawfully” raised “antinational” slogans and threw stones at security personnel and vandalised official vehicles. This is the third FIR in the December 15 violence on the AMU campus.Police have already filed two FIRs and arrested 52 people, including seven AMU students, who were booked along with 1,200-1,300 unidentified persons. SSP Akash Kulhary said 26 of the 52 were arrested from the campus.He said that FIRs were registered against them under various sections of IPC and Section 7 of the CLA Act.
