India: 31-year-old Mumbai pharmacist rapes UK-based government officer, arrested

The accused identified as Junaid Ansari befriended the victim, promised to marry her but later, took her on a drive and raped her in the car. And then, he refused to marry her.

As reported by Indian news website, indiatoday.in, a rape case has been registered against Ansari, who was arrested by the Mumbai Crime Branch and handed over to the Gaodevi police.

The incident happened in the month of July near Girgaum Chowpatty in Mumbai when Junaid had taken the woman on a drive. According to the Mumbai Police, Junaid the victim had come to India to meet her relatives.

According to the India Today report, Junaid owned a shop in Dongri from the victim's mother used to by medicines and ship them to her in the UK. The victim too had met the accused over an e-counter while buying medicines for infertility.

The victim got divorced with her husband in 2016 and had been visiting her mother in India on many occasions since then.

It was then that Junaid befriended her. The accused had promised to marry the victim.

When she was visiting him this year in July, Junaid found the woman alone and he raped her in his car.

When the victim returned to the UK and asked about their marriage plans, Junaid refused to marry her and asked her to forget whatever had happened between them.

The victim tried approaching Junaid to know what was wrong and even returned to India trying to meet him. The accused, however, refused to even talk to her.
