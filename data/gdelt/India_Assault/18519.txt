Despite opposition from some political parties to conferon Vinayak Damodar Savarkar, the Centre on Tuesday said that no formal recommendation is required.The development comes a month after Maharashtraunit said it will ask the party-ledgovernment at the Centre to confer Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, on Savarkar, popularly known asResponding to a query in Lok Sabha, the NDA said that there is no need for a formal recommendation.According to Times Now, the government has given a short reply to a question posed by the BJP MPHowever, the Centre did not give any timeline about conferring the Bharat Ratna.In a reply, the MoS Home said that from time to time they keep on receiving such recommendations, however, it is not necessary to make recommendations for Bharat Ratna. "This is the decision the government would take on its own."Many BJP leaders look up to Savarkar for inspiration although he was a member of the Hindu Mahasabha and not the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), the ideological parent of the BJP.There has been a long-standing demand that Savarkar should be conferred Bharat Ratna.Last month, slamming the BJP's proposal, Tushar Gandhi, great-grandson of Mahatma Gandhi, had said that Savarkar was the 'patron' of the Mahatma's assassins.Though Savarkar faced trial in the Gandhi assassination case, he was acquitted, but had not been pronounced innocent by the court, he claimed.The AIMIM chief Asaduddin Owaisi had also slammed the BJP for proposing the Bharat Ratna for Savarkar, saying he advocated the use of rape as a political tool.Owaisi also claimed that he was implicated by Jeevan Lal Commission of Inquiry on Gandhi’s assassination, advocated the use of rape as a political tool, criticised Shivaji for not using rape as a political tool and called himself the British’s 'most obedient servant'.In October, Chhattisgarh Chief Minister Bhupesh Baghel had said Hindutva ideologue Savarkar was part of the conspiracy to assassinate Mahatma Gandhi.
