Rajiv Gandhi Assassination case: Convict Nalini Sriharan gets 30-day parole

Rajiv Gandhi Assassination case: Convict Nalini Sriharan has been granted 30-day parole by the Madras High court in connection with the Rajiv Gandhi assassination case. She is one of the longest-serving woman prisoners.

Rajiv Gandhi Assassination case: Nalini Sriharan, one of the seven convicts in Rajiv Gandhi assassination case, has been granted 30-day parole by the Madras High Court today, July 5, 2019. Earlier in June, the court had granted permission to Nalini to appear in person on July 5 i.e. today and argue on her plea for six-month ordinary leave to make arrangements for her daughter’s wedding residing in London.

The convict was produced before a Division Bench of Justices M.M. Sundresh and M. Nirmal Kumar. Nalini, one of the longest-serving woman prisoner, has been behind the bars for over 27 years. The court has agreed to meet the demands of Nalini on the condition that she would not be allowed to meet any politicians or media personnel during the parole time.

Earlier in February too, Nalini had moved the court seeking its permission for the same saying a life convict is entitled to a one-month leave once in two years. She had said that she never availed such leave fpr more than 27 years, thus she was entitled to take a leave.

Rajiv Gandhi was assassinated in May 1991, by a Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) suicide bomber during an election rally at Sriperumbudur in Tamil Nadu.
