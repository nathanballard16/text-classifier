Uttar Pradesh: 16-year-old girl raped at gunpoint in Shamli, accused on the run

The police officials told PTI that the girl was found in an unconscious state at the accused's house. | Photo Credit: Getty

Lucknow: In a gruesome case reported from Uttar Pradesh, a 16-year-old girl was allegedly raped at gunpoint in Bhabisa village of Shamli district. The incident happened on Saturday when the survivor had gone out to relieve herself.

According to a report in the news agency PTI, the girl's father lodged a complaint against the accused, who took her into his house and raped her at gunpoint. Her father was identified as Kapil. The girl's father also alleged that the accused threatened her daughter of dire consequences if she disclosed the matter. The police officials told PTI that the girl was found in an unconscious state at the accused's house.

Kandhla Station House Officer, D K Tyagi told PTI that a case has been registered against the accused under the Indian Penal Code, Sections 376 (rape) and 506 (criminal intimidation) and Sections of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act.

However, the police added the accused has been absconding since the incident and the police is trying to nab him as soon as possible. Meanwhile, on the same, another horrifying incident was reported from Mirapur police station area, where a five-year-old girl was allegedly raped. The incident happened when the minor had gone out with her mother to a fair in the area in Muzaffarnagar district on Saturday.

Reportedly, the survivor's family lodged an official complaint and mentioned that the girl went missing from past some hours from near a temple where the fair was organised.

Meanwhile, she was allegedly raped and abandoned by the accused in a serious condition. However, the police have registered a case against unidentified persons. The minor immediately rushed to a private hospital where she is under treatment.
