3 booked for abducting 2 minor girls on promise of marriage

PATIALA: The police have registered two separate cases of abduction under sections of Protection of Children against Sexual Offenses Act against some persons for allegedly abducting two minor girls.Police claimed that in one case a 17-year-old girl was abducted and in the second, a 15-year-old girl was abducted. Both were talked into leaving their parents’ home on the pretext of marriage.Pasiana police have booked a man from Khumna village on the complaint of the girl’s father, a resident of Sultanpur village in Pasiana block. The police said that accused had acquainted himself with the minor girl for several months. The police added that girl had gone missing on the intervening night of December 3 and 4. However, the family reported the matter on late Tuesday evening. The police said that family claimed to have made efforts for days to search for their 17-year-old daughter, but in vain.“The family initially did not report the matter to the police, fearing it would bring a bad name to the family. The family had come to knew from friends of their daughter that she has been abducted on the pretext of marriage by the accused. We will soon nab the accused,” said the investigating officer. In another case, Kotwali Nabha police booked a man from Muzaffarnagar in Uttar Pradesh for abducting a 15-year-old girl on the pretext of marriage. Police said the accused was known to the girl and her family. The police said that both the accused and his brother are booked under sections of IPC as well as POCSO Act.
