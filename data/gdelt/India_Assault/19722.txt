Assam University strike over student assault

(The victim's identity has not been revealed to protect her privacy as per Supreme Court directives on cases related to sexual assault)

SILCHAR: A day-long strike called by the Assam University Students' Union crippled the functioning of the central university on Wednesday. The students demanded the arrest of two persons who allegedly assaulted three students, including a female, of the department of social work near Silchar Medical College and Hospital (SMCH) on Tuesday.The students shut the main gate of the university. No teacher, administrative staff or student could enter the campus, thus crippling the functioning of the central institute.According to an Aasu spokesperson, the three students were going back to their respective homes when the two goons, under the influence of alcohol, stopped them near the Silchar Medical College and abused and assaulted them. Besides demanding their arrest, the students' union also sought regular patrolling on the Silchar-Hailakandi road.The spokesman said that it could have been a rerun of Hyderabad incident if the locals didn't interfere.Meanwhile, Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad's (ABVP) Assam University unit submitted a memorandum to superintendent of police (Cachar), Mugdha Jyoti Dev Mahanta, demanding immediate arrest of the miscreants involved in the incident. A copy of the memorandum was sent to the governor of Assam, the chief minister, vice-chancellor of Assam University, Cachar deputy commissioner and Silchar MLA.A senior police official said that investigation is on and one of the accused has already been arrested.
