Farmers foil bid to kidnap teenage girl

Ludhiana: Two farmers foiled a bid to kidnap a 17-year-old girl when she was on way to her school in Sidhwan Bet of Jagraon. Police, quoting locals, said three car-borne miscreants were molesting the girl and trying to kidnap her, when farmers invervened and rescued her.

The police have registered a case against Sukhdeep Singh, Jassa, and their unidentified accomplice. The girl alleged that the accused had been stalking her for several days. On Wednesday, she was on her way to school. When she reached the village outskirts, the accused turned up in a white Toyota Innova and intercepted her way. The trio then allegedly molested her and tried to kidnap her. When she cried for help, two farmers — Randhir Singh and Pardeep Singh — turned up there to rescue her. According to the teenager, the accused also attacked the farmers before escaping from the spot.

ASI Gurdeep Singh said a case under sections 363 (punishment for kidnapping), 511 (punishment for attempt to commit an offence), 354-D (stalking), 34 (act done by several persons in furtherance of common intention) of the IPC, and different sections of Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (Posco) Act, was regsitered against the accused.
