Child rape: Govt backs SC’s zero tolerance policy

NEW DELHI: The Union government on Friday assured full support and assistance to the Supreme Court’s suo motu initiative to frame a zero tolerance policy towards child rape and sharpen the deterrence of the law-enforcing machinery.A bench headed by Chief Justice Ranjan Gogoi said data collated with the help of high courts showed a dismal trend relating to effectiveness of law-enforcing machinery and that police had registered more than 24,000 child rape cases across India from January to June.The bench of CJI Gogoi and Justices Deepak Gupta and Aniruddha Bose said the court would consider whether it should set up special investigation units to expeditiously probe child rape incidents and file chargesheet in trial courts.It asked senior advocate V Giri to assist the court in framing a zero tolerance policy towards child rape incidents. Solicitor general Tushar Mehta, on behalf of the Union government, supported the SC’s suo motu initiative.
