The document did not include any mention of its opposition to the metro shed at Aarey.The Shiv Sena released its manifesto, which vows to provide full meal at Rs 10 and make, among other promises on Saturday. The Uddhav Thackeray-led party is contesting 124 seats in the Maharashtra Assembly election as part of its alliance with the BJP.However, despite Sena’s opposition to the felling of trees inin Mumbai for a Metro car shed last week, the manifesto does not make any mention of this issue.Party president Uddhav Thackeray and his son and, who is making his electoral debut from Worli constituency, released the document.When asked about the Metro car shed project, Uddhav said, “We stand by our decision to oppose the proposed car shed at Aarey Colony.”Police had imposed prohibitory orders in Aarey Colony in suburban Goregaon last Saturday following protests over the cutting of trees for the car shed.Twenty nine protesters were arrested for allegedly obstructing and assaulting police personnel during the felling of trees.Aaditya Thackeray had slammed the hacking of trees and termed it as a “shameful and disgusting” act.In the manifesto, the party has promised to set up 1,000 food joints across Maharashtra, where a full meal will be made available at Rs 10.It has also promised to help farmers become debt-free and slash power tariffs of domestic consumers by 30%.Its other promises include “1rupee clinics” to be started across the state to provide health examinations and will include screenings for 200 different types of diseases.Thackeray said the manifesto prioritises issues related to women.The Shiva Sena also said that there are some open spaces in urban areas, which will be utilised for developing urban forests in cities including Mumbai and Thane.While the Sena is contesting 124 of the 288 Assembly seats, the BJP and its other allies are contesting the remaining 164 seats for the October 21 elections.
