Man sentenced to 10 years in jail for raping minor girl

Surat: A 25-year-old man was convicted of rape and abduction of a 15-year-old girl by a local court on Tuesday. The convict, who is a Limbayat resident, was sentenced to 10 years in jail by the court. The convict, currently out on bail, has been ordered to return to Lajpore Central Jail once his bail ends on May 22.

Samir alias Ali Hussain alias Chhotu alias Prince Mirza was convicted of raping a retired police officer’s daughter in August 2013. The girl had eloped with the convict who took her to his native place in Prayagraj of Uttar Pradesh. He converted her to Islam and got married to her and repeatedly raped her, police investigation revealed. The court convicted the man of rape and kidnapping under Indian Penal Code and Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act. He was also convicted under Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Amendment Act.

The rape survivor in her complaint said the convict had developed friendship with her by changing his name. The convict came in contact with the girl as she used to come for tuition in the area where he lived. He used to threaten to kill her parents and kidnap her brother if she didn’t accept his friendship. The convict also gave a mobile phone to the rape survivor and used to talk to her often.

The man took the girl to Mumbai and other places in Maharashtra after a police pl- aint was registered against him at Limbayat police stati-on. Police nabbed the duo from Nagpur and brought them to the city. Samir alias Ali Hussain alias Chhotu alias Prince Mirza was later arrested.
