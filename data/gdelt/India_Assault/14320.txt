UP trader death: 100 booked for assaulting cops during protest

NOIDA: More than 100 people were booked for allegedly assaulting policemen and vandalising police vehicles during a protest at Jewar over the death of a local trader. The trader, Deepak Singh, from Aligarh in Uttar Pradesh , was allegedly thrashed by four men after an argument broke out between them when they were drinking on August 18, a police spokesperson said.“Following the attack, Deepak was referred to Safdarjung hospital in Delhi where he succumbed to his injuries on Thursday,” he said. When his body was brought to Jewar, 100-150 people from Baina village started protesting and demanded police action against the accused, the spokesperson said. “Police reached the spot and tried to pacify the protesters. As the officers spoke to the villagers, some people from the crowd attacked police vehicles, broke the wind shield of one police SUV and injured the personnel,” he said.“A case was registered on Thursday night against 115 people. They have been charged under relevant sections for blocking road, violence and assaulting policemen,” he said.An FIR under IPC Section 308 (culpable homicide) was also lodged over Singh’s killing. The accused in the case have been identified as Satish, Munendra, Kuldeep and Ravindra. Two of them have been arrested.
