JUSTICE - Minor rape survivor allowed to abort 22-week foetus

Jaipur: The Rajasthan high court on Tuesday allowed a minor rape survivor to abort her foetus of 22 weeks. The court announced the order after the survivor's health report was submitted by the Medical Board of Medical College, Udaipur.

According to a report in the Hindustan Times (HT), the minor girl hailed from Chittorgarh district and her father filed a petition in the the High Court. He sought permission to terminate her daughter's pregnancy as soon as possible. Reportedly, the petitioner told the HT that the minor was allegedly raped by a man named Veni ram Suthar around 5-6 months back.

Reportedly, the counsel for petitioner Ankur Mathur told HT, the medical examination of the girl revealed that she had become pregnant as a result of forceful sexual intercourse.

According to Section 3 of the Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971, a pregnancy exceeding 20 weeks cannot be terminated except with the consent of the pregnant woman.

Meanwhile, the victim's father has registered an FIR under section 376 of the Indian Penal Code and Sections of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences. However, on the basis of the medical report, justice Ashok Kumar Gaur allowed the petitioner to abort her 22-week foetus.
