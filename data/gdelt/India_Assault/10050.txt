Delhi youth lynched on suspicion of stealing mobile phone

The shopkeepers allowed one of the accused to leave and detained the victim. (Representative image) | Photo Credit: Getty Images

New Delhi: A 20-year-old man has been beaten to death on suspicion of stealing a mobile phone from a shop in Prem Nagar area of Rohini. The incident occurred on Saturday evening when one Amit, who runs a mobile phone shop on Mubarakpur Road thrashed the youth for allegedly stealing the phone.

The victim has not yet been identified. According to a report in Times of India, the victim allegedly stole the phone and a CCTV camera DVR from Amit's shop last month. The shopkeeper later managed to acquire the CCTV footage showing the youth stealing the gadgets.

The report further said when the victim once again visited Amit's shop along with his accomplice, the duo was chased and caught by the shopkeeper. The two youths were brought to the shop where they were allegedly brutally thrashed by Amit and other shopkeepers.

Police have registered a case under Section 304 of IPC (culpable homicide not amounting to murder).

According to DCP (Rohini) SD Mishra, a preliminary probe has revealed that the unidentified victim was caught by the shop owner on Saturday evening. The shopkeepers allowed the accomplice of the youth to leave and detained him instead, thinking his parents would come to pay up for the stolen phone. But the youth fell unconscious after thrashing. He was rushed to the hospital where he was declared brought dead. The police were later informed about the matter.

Police have procured the CCTV footage of the area and recorded statements of the witnesses. Police said the body of the youth has been kept at the mortuary and a postmortem will be done to identify the cause of the death.
