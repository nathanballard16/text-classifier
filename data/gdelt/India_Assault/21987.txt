Delhi: 25-year-old man sets self ablaze near India Gate

A 25-year-old man set himself ablaze near India Gate in New Delhi Wednesday evening. A police team stationed nearby rushed the man to a hospital where he is being treated. The man -- a native of Odisha -- set himself on fire on Rajpath, the ceremonial road that runs from Rashtrapati Bhavan on Raisina Hill to India Gate.

A Police Control Room van of the Dehi Police stationed nearby rushed the man to the Ram Manohar Lohia Hospital where he is being treated. Doctors at the hospital said he has suffered 90 per cent burns.

The man, identified as 25-year-old Kartik Maher, set himself ablaze near India Gate at a time when the area around the monument has been witnessing protests against the controversial Citizenship Amendment Act.

News agency PTI, quoting police however reported that the man was not linked to the protest against CAA. "His brother told us that he is mentally unstable," Deputy Commissioner of Police Eish Singhal was quoted as saying by the agency.

According to hospital authorities, Maher's condition is critical.

"The man has suffered almost 90 per cent burns and is unconscious. He has been admitted in the emergency ward and is being attended to by doctors. He is critical," Dr Minakshi Bharadwaj, medical superintendent of the RML hospital, said.
