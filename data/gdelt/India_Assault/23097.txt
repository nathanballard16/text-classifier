Mumbai: Bar waitress found dead under suspicious circumstances in Dahisar

The body of the 32-year-old was found on Monday.

A bar waitress was, suspiciously, found dead at her rented home in Dahisar East area of Mumbai on Sunday.

The police, however, said that they did not find any injury marks on her body and the post-mortem report did not reveal about the cause of death.

Identified as Rosina Shaikh, the deceased was residing at the flat with her live-in partner. However, he was in Hyderabad when she died.

The 32-year-old deceased initially lived with her husband in Sanpada, Navi Mumbai but she moved out to SRA building in Dahisar after separating. Shaikh did not have children.

According to a report in TimesNow, Shaikh came home around 11 pm on Saturday and went off to sleep. When her maid came in the morning, Shaikh didn't open the door and the maid thought she is not at home. She then again came at 3 pm. When Shaikh didn't open the door again, the maid called her landlord and they opened the door.

The landlord and the maid found Shaikh's body lying on her bed. At first, they called a doctor and when he declared her dead, police was informed. The body was sent for an autopsy.

According to a police officer who is familiar with the case, a drink was found in the house during the investigation.

The police officer said that the drink might have been laced with a substance which led to Shaikh's death. The drink has been sent to a forensic science laboratory in Kalina.

Shaikh's live-in partner is also being interrogated. A case of accidental death has been registered.
