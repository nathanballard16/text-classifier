Ludhiana: Teen stabs man after he flings his turban; arrested

The victim was referred to civil hospital in Ludhiana where he succumbed to his injuries (Representative Image). | Photo Credit: Getty Images

Ludhiana: A teen allegedly stabbed a 27-year-old man to death with his 'kirpan' in Ludhiana. The incident took place on Saturday, June 9 at Indira Colony, Mullanpur Dakha. The youth allegedly stabbed the deceased after he flung off his turban.

The victim was identified as Sandeep alias Gaggu. A resident of Indira Colony, the victim was a truck driver. The accused was identified as Mani Singh alias Bheem.

The victim's father told Hindustan Times that locals informed him about the incident. After he was informed, the victim's father took him to a government hospital in Mullanpur. The victim was later referred to civil hospital in Ludhiana where he succumbed to his injuries.

According to Dakha DSP Gurbans Singh Bains, the victim had flung off the accused's turban following which, the latter stabbed him. The accused was arrested and booked under Section 302 (murder) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC). The police are yet to ascertain the reason behind the incident.
