Hundreds protest against rape, murder of Hyd vet

Varanasi: Large scale protests erupted in the city on Saturday against the brutal rape and murder of a veterinarian in Hyderabad in the early hours of Thursday.Protesters demanded death penalty for the accused.Activists of the Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad (ABVP) took out a procession in Maidagin area and staged a massive demonstration.They held a meeting before setting the effigy of Telangana government on fire and demanded capital punishment to the accused.ABVP activists demanded that the Telangana government should ensure trial in fast track court.Students of Banaras Hindu University (BHU) also took out a protest march and staged a demonstration at the main gate of BHU.Members of Aagman, a voluntary organization working for girl child protection, also paid tribute to the veterinarian ahead of Ganga aarti at Dashaswamedh Ghat in the evening. Activists performed ‘Shanti Path’ and ‘Deepdan’ in her memory. Several other organisations also held protests.The veterinary doctor, who worked in a Telangana state-run hospital, was raped and set on fire by four men, all lorry workers, on the outskirts of Hyderabad on November 28.Telangana cops arrested four accused on Friday in connection with the heinous crime which triggered a national outrage.
