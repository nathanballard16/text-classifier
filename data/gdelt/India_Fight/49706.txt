Fire breaks out at SBI regional office

Computers, ACs, records turn into ashes | Short-circuit likely causeSukhmeet BhasinTribune News ServiceBathinda, October 30A major fire broke out at the regional office of the State Bank of India (SBI) on the Amrik Singh road here around 5 am on Wednesday. It seems that the reason behind the fire could be a short-circuit, but officially it has not been confirmed yet. US Gupta, regional manager, SBI, said, “It seems that the fire broke out due to a short-circuit. There is no damage to public as the office does not have any direct link with customers. Earlier in 2013, a fire had broken out on the first floor of an Axis Bank branch on the Mall road here.
