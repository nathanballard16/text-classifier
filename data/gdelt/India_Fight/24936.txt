Rain brings much-needed relief to city

Light to moderate showers swept Hyderabad and other parts of Telangana on Saturday evening, providing denizens the much-needed relief from the sweltering summer heat.

After a sizzling day at 42 degrees Celsius in Hyderabad, the rain and thunderstorm in the evening came as a welcome relief for the citizens.

The showers that began around 7.30 p.m., were initially limited to brief spells across a few areas, including Kukatpally, Qutbullahpur, Khairatabad, Begumpet, part of Alwal, Malkajgiri, Uppal, L.B.Nagar and Kothapet. The rain later spread to various localities, including Nampally, Afzalgunj, Abids, Koti, Vanasthalipuram, Hayatnagar, Amberpet, Nagole, Alwal, Tarnaka, Habsiguda, Banjara Hills, Jubilee Hills, Punjagutta, Begumpet, Secunderabad, Musheerabad, Jeedimetla, Kompalli, Suraram, Neredmet, Malkajgiri, Kukatpally, Hydernagar, Allwyn Colony, and Patancheru.

At 11.30 p.m., the total rainfall received by the city was recorded by Met department as 4.9 mm.

The department predicted similar weather for Sunday too, with day temperature reaching a maximum of 41 degrees Celsius, and the night time temperatures 28 degrees. The officials attributed the unseasonal rain to formation of upper air trough extending up to 0.9 kilometres.

The rain flooded roads and created traffic congestion at many places. A construction worker Vadthyavath Balu (42) was killed when a wall of a corporate college at Annojiguda near Ghatkesar fell on him. Power supply was disrupted and drain water overflowed on roads.

At Redyala village of Mahabubabad mandal, 25 sheep were struck dead by lightning . Hailstorm hit villages of Bhongir mandal. Trees fell under the impact of wind in Khammam district.
