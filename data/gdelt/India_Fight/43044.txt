Punjab: Revenue officer attacked on first day of duty; hospitalised

LUDHIANA: A revenue officer has been attacked by four assailants on his first day of joining in Thareeke village, Ludhiana district of Punjab on Tuesday morning. The accused assailants, who came on two bikes, attacked officer with sharp edged weapons and escaped.On hearing screams, nearby people rushed to the spot and found the man injured. Following which, they took him to a private hospital on Ferozepur road. The condition of injured is stated to be out of danger.The injured has been identified as Deepak Singla, 27, of Sardulgarh, district Mansa. Earlier he was posted is village Bhundri of Ludhiana Rural after which he was transferred to Thareeke.Inspector Sukhdev Singh, SHO at Sadar police station, said, “The man has sustained injuries on his head, neck, arm, hand and leg. He was attacked several times by sharp edged weapons in his office. His neck injury is quite deep.” aAdditional Deputy Commissioner of Police (ADCP-2) Jaskiranjit Singh said that, during initial inquiry it has been identified that the assailants have argued with victim before attacking him. It seems that accused are known to victim and personal rivalry is the reason behind the attack as on first day nobody of duty nobody can be attacked brutally.The Sadar police have registered a case against unidentified accused under section of 307 (attempt to murder), 353 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty), 186 (obstructing public servant in discharge of public functions), 332 (voluntarily causing hurt to deter public servant from his duty), 333 (whoever voluntarily causes grievous hurt to any person being a public servant) and 34 (Act done by several persons in furtherance of common intention) of IPC.
