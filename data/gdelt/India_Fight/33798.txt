Ajay Devgn to join Sanjay Dutt in Hyderabad for 'Bhuj: The Pride of India' shoot

Abhishek Dudhaiya's directorial debut Bhuj: The Pride of India has an incredible cast which includes Ajay Devgn, Sanjay Dutt, Parineeti Chopra, Sonakshi Sinha, Rana Daggubati and Ammy Virk. The film is based on a true story, where Ajay will be seen playing the role of Squadron Leader Vijay Karnik, who was in charge of the Bhuj airport during the 1971 Indo-Pak war. The movie is produced by T-Series.

It was recently reported that Sanjay kickstarted the shoot in Hyderabad on June 25, 2019. Soon after that, Sonakshi joined him at Ramoji Film City. A Mumbai Mirror report suggested that both shot for some scenes and also a folk song while in Hyderabad. In the film, Sanjay will be seen as a civilian and Sonakshi as a social worker. Now as per reports in the tabloid, Ajay will be starting the shoot on July 11, 2019, as he has returned from his family vacation.

Talking about it, a source said to the tabloid, "He begins his portions with Sanju on July 11 in Hyderabad. After that, Ajay will fly to Gujarat where a major chunk will be shot with him."

Earlier, Bhushan had said about Sanjay and Ajay, "Sanjay and Ajay’s characters are powerful and are the force behind the story. The audience will see them together in a different avatar."

While a source talked by Parineeti and Ammy, "Parineeti will join the shoot in a couple of months and Ammy will start in September. It’s an ensemble film spearheaded by Ajay and every character plays a pivotal role in taking the story forward."
