Advancement of southwest monsoon to create 'favourable' weather conditions: IMD

Representational image | Photo Credit: ANI

New Delhi: Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) on Friday said that favourable weather conditions will be created as southwest monsoon advances into remaining parts of the central Arabian sea. According to the IMD bulletin, significant weather changes are likely to be seen in Telangana, Konkan, Maharashtra, Odisha, West Bengal, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Bihar, Jharkhand and east Uttar Pradesh over the next 72 hours.

In its latest bulletin, the IMD said that conditions are becoming favourable as 'further advance of southwest monsoon into the remaining parts of central Arabian sea, Karnataka, Maharashtra, some parts of Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, remaining parts of Tamilnadu, Bay of Bengal, some more parts of West Bengal and Sikkim, Bihar, Jharkhand and Odisha during the first half of the next week'.

"The Southwest Monsoon has further advanced into some parts of Central Arabian Sea, remaining parts of Kerala & Coastal Karnataka, some parts of interior Karnataka, south Konkan & Goa, south Madhya Maharashtra, some more parts of Tamilnadu, most parts of Southwest Bay of Bengal, some more parts of Bay of Bengal and remaining parts of northeastern states and some more parts of West Bengal," IMD said in a release.

Meanwhile, rough seas and strong winds are expected to be experienced over the central Arabian Sea in regions such as Lakshadweep Islands and around the north of Bay of Bengal. Earlier today, parts of Delhi received showers bringing respite from the scorching heat. Cloud cover is expected to keep the weather pleasant in Delhi and the surrounding region over the weekend, the IMD had earlier said.
