Journalist shot at while driving in East Delhi; police not ruling out personal enmity

Prima facie it appears to be a case of a family dispute, Singh added | Photo Credit: ANI

New Delhi: A journalist working with a Noida-based company was shot at by a group of unidentified masked men on Sunday. The incident happened in East Delhi's Vasundhara Enclave.

Around 12:30 AM, when the woman was driving her Hyundai i20, multiple shots were fired at the journalist. According to news agency ANI, the journalist said, two masked assailants first threw eggs at the front glass of the car and when she did not stop, they fired two shots at her. Reportedly, it happened near Dharamshila Narayana Superspeciality Hospital.

The Deputy Commissioner of Police (East), Jasmeet Singh, told media that she has received bullet injuries in the arm and is out of danger. Prima facie it appears to be a case of a family dispute, Singh added. However, the investigation in the case underway. The police are trying to ascertain the identity of the assailants.
