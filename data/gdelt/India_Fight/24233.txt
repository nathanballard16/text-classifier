Head constable found dead at home

Preliminary enquiry reveals he was upset over family disputes

A 51-year-old Delhi police head constable allegedly shot himself dead with his service pistol at his residence in Hari Nagar police colony on Sunday night. No note has been recovered, the police said.

Deputy Commissioner of Police (West) Monika Bhardwaj said that the deceased has been identified as head constable Satish Kumar, posted in Vikaspuri police station.

He allegedly shot himself at 11.30 p.m. after which he was rushed to a nearby hospital by his family where he was declared brought dead, she said.

The police said that his son, working with the IAF, told them that Kumar had dinner with his family after which everyone went to their respective rooms.

A gunshot was heard after which the family members rushed to his room where he was found lying in a pool of blood.

“He was absent from work since May 3 and he had not informed anyone about his absence. We are enquiring how he had the service pistol in his possession,” Ms. Bhardwaj said.

Officers, after duty, are expected to submit their service revolvers in the police station.

The police said that preliminary enquiry revealed that he was upset over some family disputes.
