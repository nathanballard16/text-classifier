Saket court murder case: Delhi police lay trap to arrest 3 men for killing gangster

Accordingly, a team was formed and a trap was laid at the specific place of information. | Photo Credit: Getty Images

New Delhi: In a case reported from the national capital, a week after the alleged murder of a local gangster during a gang war near the Saket court, the Delhi Police has arrested three accused in the case on Monday.

The accused were identified as Badal, Satender and Asfak Ahmad. Reportedly, they were allegedly hatching and executing the conspiracy of murder of local gangster Prince alias Bihari.

The police have confiscated four mobile phones and one motorcycle which was used in the alleged crime. According to news agency ANI, the Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP) Crime, G Ram Gopal Naik said that during the interrogation, it was revealed that victim Prince had a rivalry with Rohit Choudhary as he recently threatened and abused him publicly. Following this insult, Choudhary was trying to eliminate him.

Reportedly, in the first two attempts, Prince had escaped their clutches but the third time Rohit and his associates made a full-proof plan to eliminate him. Naik further added that on May 6 Prince, who had come to Saket Court was being followed by Badal, Asfak Ahmad and Satender. Later, Prince was fired upon indiscriminately after which he died.

Reportedly, the information was received on May 12 that a criminal named Badal, who was involved in the crime would come to Government Boys Senior Secondary in Khanpur to cast his vote. Accordingly, a team was formed and a trap was laid at the specific place of information.

Meanwhile, at about 1 PM, one person who was later identified as Badal was apprehended by the police on the basis of the information received. And later two of his other associates Satender and Asfak Ahmed who were also involved in the crime were also arrested.

However, further investigation in the matter is underway.
