Conflicting narratives surround India’s bombing raid on Pakistan

Both India and Pakistan were frantically spinning the bombing raid for their respective audiences last night. India said 300 militants were killed; Pakistan mockingly posted pictures of bomb craters in an empty forest.

The conflicting narratives may yet allow the two governments an honourable way out of immediate escalation, although India’s general election in May adds an unpredictable factor to this crisis.

India and Pakistan have fought four wars since independence and despite the constant sabre-rattling, neither can afford another full-scale confrontation now that they each possess about 140 nuclear warheads and the missiles to deliver them.

Less clear is the way out of a wider cycle of a terrorist strike or raid by jihadists followed by Indian retaliation.
