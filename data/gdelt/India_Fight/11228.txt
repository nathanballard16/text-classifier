On Wednesday at least one Indian warplane was shot down by Pakistani forces after crossing the international border. The confrontation followed an Indian aerial attack on suspected militant camps in Pakistan earlier in the week. India is demanding the release of one of its pilots who was captured.

Speaking to RT Sultan Mehmood Hali, formerly a group commander in the Pakistani Air Force, warned: “If pushed against the wall Pakistan will be forced to retaliate with nuclear weapons, which could lead to very serious destruction in the region.”

However the military veteran insisted Pakistan does not want all-out war with India.

He commented: “We don't want this to escalate to mutually assured destruction.

“India is the superior power. Its armed forces are three times the size of Pakistan's. Its economy is much broader. It can sustain a lot of hits.

“Pakistan, being the weaker country, will be on the receiving end.”
