Amid rising anger over attacks onacross the country, Prime Ministeron Friday asked states to take strict action against those indulging in such activities.The Prime Minister's remark comes a day after two Kashmiri vendors selling dry fruits were thrashed by a group of saffron-clad men inAddressing a rally in Uttar Pradesh's Kanpur, Modi also said that the country needs to move ahead with the mantra of unity.Referring to the assault on Kashmiris, Modi also said that the Uttar Pradesh BJP government led by Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath had acted swiftly against the miscreants."I request all other states to take stern action against those trying to indulge in such activities. We have to end terrorism. We have to move ahead with the mantra of unity," Modi said.Meanwhile, Union Home Ministersaid that if somebody conspires and troubles our Kashmiri students then it's the responsibility of BJP workers to stand with them.Earlier in the day, Congress President Rahul Gandhi hailed the person who saved Kashmiri vendors from being beaten-up in Lucknow and said that the country belongs to citizens from every corner.The police has arrested four people in connection with the incident. The accused have been identified as Bajrang Sonkar, Amar, Himanshu and Anirrudh.Sonkar, who was arrested earlier, is affiliated to the Vishwa Hindu Dal (VHD) and has a dozen criminal cases registered against him, including one of murder.There have been reports from different parts of the country targeting Kashmiris in the aftermath of the Pulwama terror attack that killed more than 40 CRPF personnel.
