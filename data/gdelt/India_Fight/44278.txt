Man found murdered, cops in dark

Ludhiana: A man possibly in his 30s was found murdered in the fields near Khasi Khurd and Kudyani Kalan villages at Meherban on Thursday morning.

Police said Surinder Singh, a farmer who lived near the Khasi Khurd and Kudyani Kalan villages, first noticed the body and called up Meherban police at 8.30am. According to Surinder, the man was lying in a pool of blood near an electricity pole close to the fields of his brother.

Police said the investigation suggested that the victim might have been hit with an iron rod used to change tyres. The murder possibly was committed on Wednesday night. Cops also recovered a liquor bottle and a few plastic glasses from the spot, suggesting that the assailants and the victim well acquainted and were drinking before the crime.

Meherban SHO Kulwant Singh Malhi said police have registered a case under Sections 302 (murder), 201 (Causing disappearance of evidence of offence, or giving false information to screen offender) and 34 (Acts done by several persons in furtherance of common intention) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) against unidentified persons. He added that it appears that more than one person was involved in the crime.

“As we have got no document from the body, we have circulated the picture of the victim and are making announcement from religious places to get some clues about his identity,” he added.

The body has been taken the mortuary at Ludhiana civil hospital where it will be kept for three days for identification.
