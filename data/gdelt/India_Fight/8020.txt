One Army jawan killed, five more feared dead in an avalanche in Himachal Pradesh’s Kinnaur district

A picture of the incident site | Photo Credit: ANI

Shimla (Himachal Pradesh): An Army jawan was killed and five more were feared dead after they were hit by an avalanche on the Sino-India border in Namgya region of Himachal Pradesh’s Kinnaur district. Five jawans of the Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) were also injured in the incident.

The search operation for the missing jawans was being carried out by the ITBP and the district police. PTI reported that the six Army jawans belonged to the JK Rifles unit.

Kinnaur Deputy Commissioner Gopal Chand said the body of one of the jawans had been found, but they were not able to locate the five missing jawans. Officials said the missing five were also feared dead.

The avalanche occurred at around 11 am near Shipkala on the Sino-India border, the official said. According to Chand, several ITBP jawans had also got trapped in the avalanche but they were rescued.

Meanwhile, the Army jawan whose body was recovered from the snow has been identified as Rakesh Kumar (41) of Ghumarpur village in Bilaspur district of Himachal Pradesh.

It was reported that 16 jawans were sent to the Shipki La border post from Namagya to repair a damaged water supply line. They were hit by an avalanche and six of them were buried alive.

An Army spokesperson was quoted as saying that the search and rescue operation would not end till the last man was retrieved.
