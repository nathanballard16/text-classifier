Jawan killed, three injured as Pakistan violates ceasefire in Poonch

JAMMU: An Army jawan was killed and three others injured in the latest ceasefire violation along the Line of Control (LoC) by Pakistan in Jammu and Kashmir’s Poonch district on Monday.Following India’s airstrikes on Balakot on February 26, Pakistan has violated ceasefire 513 times along the LoC in J&K till April 12, in which 10 people — including four security personnel — were killed and 45 others, mostly civilians, were injured.“Around 5pm, Pakistan resorted to unprovoked mortar shelling and small arms firing, targeting forward posts along the LoC in Poonch’s Doda battalion area. Lance Naik Mohammad Jawed (28) was critically wounded in the fire exchange and later succumbed to his injuries,” defence spokesperson Lt Col Devender Anand said.“Jawed belonged to Marrar village in Bihar ’s Khagaria district and is survived by his wife. He was a brave and sincere soldier. The nation will always remain indebted to him for the supreme sacrifice,” Anand added.
