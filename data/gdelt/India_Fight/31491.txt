Delhi: Two masked men shoot at journo's car

A woman journalist was shot at allegedly by a group of unidentified men in Ashok Nagar on Saturday night while she was driving. According to reports, the woman journalist Mitali Chandola, who works with a media organisation in Noida, was driving her Hyundai i20 car around 12:30 am on Sunday when the incident occurred.

The report said that her car was overtaken by a Maruti Swift car following which the group of men fired two shots at her and even hurled eggs on the windshield of the car.

At around 12.30 am, another car suddenly overtook her and started throwing eggs on her car and when she did not stop her car, the group of men opened opened fire at her. She was hit by a bullet in her arm.

The accused after opening fire ran away from spot. Deputy Commissioner of Police (East Delhi) Jasmeet Singh said, "Police has registered case under section 307 of attempt to murder. Police is examining the CCTV cameras installed near the crime spot."

"Two masked assailants first threw eggs at the front glass of my car and when I did not stop, they fired two shots at me," the complainant said.

Police said she has been living separate from her husband. Police said they are probing all angle including that of personal enmity.

They are also probing the involvement of her husband in the incident, police said, adding they are looking for CCTV footage if available to establish the sequence of event. Police added they are trying to ascertain the identify of the accused and further investigation is underway.
