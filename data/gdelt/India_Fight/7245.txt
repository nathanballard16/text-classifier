Pulwama encounter: 5 key points

The encounter site was 10 km away from the site where the 40 CRPF soldiers were killed. | Photo Credit: Twitter

New Delhi: Following the gruesome and cowardly act that killed atleast 40 Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) officers in Pulwama, Jammu and Kashmir, the Pakistan Army positions across the border are seeing heightened activities. Also, as an aftermath of the attack, on Monday, four Army officers including a major were killed during an encounter. Along with Army men, two terrorists and a civilian were also killed. Here's all you need to know about the encounter:

1. The dreaded Jaish-e-Mohammad commander and a key aide of Pakistani terrorist Masood Azhar was killed in the encounter. The second terrorist was identified as Ghazi Rasheed.

2. The four army men killed in the encounter belonged to the 55 Rashtriya Rifles and were identified as Major Vibhuti Shankar Dhoundiya, Hav Sheo Ram, Sep Ajay Kumar and Sep Hari Singh.

#ArmyCdrNC and all ranks salute the supreme sacrifice of our brave officer and soldiers & offer deepest condolences to the families. @adgpi @PIB_India @SpokespersonMoD pic.twitter.com/qo8TzBJF89 — NorthernComd.IA (@NorthernComd_IA) February 18, 2019

3. The four soldiers were killed during an overnight encounter with Jaish terrorists in Pulwama. The encounter site was 10 km away from the site where the 40 CRPF soldiers were killed.

4. A civilian was also killed in the encounter along with two terrorists.

5. The encounter operation was launched after a tip-off, which stated that three Jaish terrorists, including the handler of the Pulwama suicide bomber Adil Ahmed Dhar, were hiding in the Pinglan area. Along with the deceased, two Indian soldiers have been injured.
