Christchurch mosques shooting: Hyderabad man injured in attack

HYDERABAD: An Indian man from Hyderabad was among the 20 people injured in shooting incidents at two New Zealand mosques on Friday.The man, identified as Ahmed Iqbal Jahangir, was rushed to a hospital after the attack where he underwent surgery.Jahangir’s parents are residents of Amberpet in Hyderabad. He went to New Zealand 15 years ago and owns a restaurant in Christchurch Sources close to the family said they have been informed about the surgery but are not sure about his present health condition.MIM leader and Hyderabad MP Asaduddin Owaisi urged TRS working president K T Rama Rao to help Jahangir’s family to travel to New Zealand. He said a video form Christchurch showed that Ahmed Jahangir was shot during the incident.Meanwhile, Indian high commissioner to New Zealand Sanjiv Kohli has said that nine people of Indian nationality/origin are reportedly missing following the attack.“As per updates received from multiple sources, there are nine missing persons of Indian national/origin. Official confirmation still awaited. Huge crime against humanity. Our prayers are with their families,” Kohli tweeted.
