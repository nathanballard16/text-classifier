NIA takes over YS Jagan Mohan Reddy attack case

The attacker used the kind of blade used in cock fights to strike the YSR Congress leader's shoulder. (Photo: ANI)

In a swift move, the Central government has informed an Andhra Pradesh high court that it asked the the National Investigation Agency (NIA) to investigate the attack on YSR Congress Party chief YS Jagan Mohan Reddy. Reddy was stabbed with a surgical blade at the high security Visakhapatnam Airport in Andhra Pradesh in October last year.

After the attack, the Andhra Pradesh government constituted a Special Investigation Team (SIT) to probe the attack and murder conspiracy of the opposition leader and found that it carried out for publicity and not with the intention to kill Reddy. Police also seized two knives from the accused Srinivas Rao, who was working as a waiter at the airport and arrested immediately.

The opposition party of the state and alleged the ruling party for hatched the conspiracy to kill Reddy, knocking the door of Centre and the President to seek an investigation by a central agency.

On December 31, the Ministry of Home Affairs passed an order asking the anti-terror agency to register an FIR and investigate the case, which took place at a place administered by the central government. On January 1, Hyderabad unit of the NIA registered the case and have started investigating the case.
