Manohar Parrikar no more: Lesser known facts about the 'CM of Commons'

Goa Chief Minister Manohar Parrikar breathed his last at his residence near Panaji on Sunday

Goa Chief Minister Manohar Parrikar breathed his last at his residence near Panaji on Sunday.

The 63-year-old minister was battling a pancreatic ailment since last year.

Following his demise, the Central government has announced national mourning on March 18. The National Flag will fly at half-mast in the national capital and capitals of all States and UTs. The state funeral will be accorded to Manohar Parrikar.

Grief-stricken President of India, Ramnath Kovind while condoling the death of Parrikar took to his Twitter and wrote, "Extremely sorry to hear of the passing of Shri Manohar Parrikar, Chief Minister of Goa after an illness borne with fortitude and dignity. An epitome of integrity and dedication in public life, his service to the people of Goa and of India will not be forgotten".

A three-time Chief Minister of Goa, Manohar Parrikar had been in and out of the hospitals since February last year. It was in October that the state government made a formal announcement about the BJP leader suffering from a pancreatic ailment. He had undergone treatment in the United States as well as Delhi.

Parrikar took charge as the Chief Minister on March 14, 2017, and held the office till his death on March 17.

He had previously served as the Chief Minister of the state from 2000 to 2002, 2002 to 2005 and from 2012 to 2014.

Manohar Parrikar, an IIT-B alumnus, has also served as the Defence Minister. His nearly three-year tenure as the defence minister was eventful.
