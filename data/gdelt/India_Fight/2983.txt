Lance Naik Nazir Ahmad Wani to be posthumously conferred with Ashoka Chakra

Lance Naik Wani is survived by a wife and two children. (Photo: Twitter/@ShivAroor)

Lance Naik Nazir Ahmad Wani, who laid down his life during an anti-terror operation at Batagund village in J&K's Shopian in November last year, will be posthumously conferred with the Ashoka Chakra.

The Ashoka Chakra is India's highest peacetime gallantry medal.

The soldier was once a terrorist, but gave up those activities and joined the Territorial Army. He was in the 34 Rashtriya Rifles when intelligence was received regarding the presence of heavily armed Hizb-ul and Lashkar terrorists at Batagund village.

Lance Naik Wani closed in on a house where another terrorist was hiding.

Wani encountered the terrorist in a hand to hand combat situation. He eliminated the terrorist, but was critically wounded.

He later succumbed to his injuries.

The 38-year-old resident, who had joined the Army in 2004, had been awarded Sena Medal for gallantry twice.

Wani is survived by a wife and two children.
