Switzerland’s FTA shares financial information with India, a major milestone in Government’s fight against black money

In a major milestone in Government’s battle against black money, India has received first chunk of Swiss bank account details of its nationals under a new automatic information exchange pact.

The next exchange is likely to take place in September 2020, said a Federal Tax Administration (FTA) spokesperson. India is among 75 countries with which Switzerland’s FTA exchanged the information on financial accounts within the framework of global standards on Automatic Exchange of Information (AEOI).

The exchanged details include identification, account and financial information, including name, address, state of residence and tax identification number, along with the information related to the financial institution, account balance and capital income.

The information exchanged is being strictly governed as per the confidentiality clauses.
