Bengaluru: A 34-year-old man who stole a mobile phone from a teenager was allegedly beaten to death by the latter's brother along with another man in Bharathinagar area of the city on Sunday evening.

The victim was identified as Mohammed Irshad, a resident of Shivajinagar and a flower seller who succumbed to injuries on Monday. The accused were identified as 24-year-old Mani and 20-year-old Shaktivelu both residents of Shivajinagar. According to a report by The Times of India, during the preliminary investigation in the matter, the police found out that Irshad had snatched Mani's younger brother Nelson's phone on Sunday evening at around 5 pm.

Irshad along with his friends had also assaulted Nelson. After Nelson narrated his ordeal to his brother, Mani along with his friend rushed to spot to confront Irshad about the harassment.

Reportedly, Irshad whipped out a knife and attempted to attack the duo. The police told a media officer that while trying to empower Irshad, Mani snatched the knife from his hand and attacked Irshad with the same knife. They reportedly stabbed him before fleeing the spot.

Onlookers rushed Irshad to a local hospital but he succumbed to injuries on Monday. The police officials are now questioning Nelson and the accused to know why Irshad snatched his phone and other details about the incident. However, the accused have been booked under relevant sections of the Indian Penal Code and further probe is underway.
