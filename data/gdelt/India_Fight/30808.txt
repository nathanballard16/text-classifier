J&K police foils a major terror attack with arrest of five Hizb-ul-Mujahideen associates

Having arrested five people, believed to be associates of terrorists of terror outfit Hizb-ul-Mujahideen, Jammu and Kashmir Police have said a major terror attack has been prevented. The arrest of all the five men was made on the basis of specific inputs.

Arrested Hizbul terrorists, identified as Sameer Ahmed Bhat, Aqib Nazir Rathar, Faisal Farooq Ahanger, Rayees Ahmed Ganai and Amir Majeed Wani were planning an IED attack on police and security forces in Shopian region of Jammu and Kashmir.

A sophisticated IED has also been recovered by the police from their possession.

Apart from this, the intelligence agencies have received fresh inputs of Pakistan-based terror outfits like Jaish-e-Mohammad (JeM) and Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT) planning to carry out attacks on military installations and convoys of security forces in the Baramulla sector in north Kashmir and on Srinagar-Kupwara Highway. Also the agencies have warned of attacks on security personnel near the Line of Control forces.
