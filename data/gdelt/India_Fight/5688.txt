Police on high alert as nurses threaten to jump

PATIALA: The city police are on high alert after the nurses, who climbed the building of Government Rajinder hospital seven days ago, threatened to jump if the government failed to meet their demands.Taking precautions, the cops have installed a net to catch protesters. Besides, mattresses have been placed on the areas where the nurses can dare to jump.One of the police officials, requesting anonymity, said that they had plans to take control of the protesters at night.Six protesters, including president Karamjit Kaur , had climbed the hospital building on the first day of their protest. Later five got sick and were hospitalised. Following this, some other protesters climbed the building.It has been learned that police have plans to take the protesters down forcibly.
