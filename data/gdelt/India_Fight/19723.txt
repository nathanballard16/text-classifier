Man Attacked By Neighbours In Delhi's Parliament Street Area

All the three accused were arrested and a case has been registered against them. (Representational)

A 40-year-old man was injured after he was allegedly attacked with wooden sticks by his three neighbours in New Delhi's Parliament Street area, the police said on Tuesday.

The man, identified as Harish Rawat, is a resident of Vithal Bhai Patel House in New Delhi, they said.

According to a senior police officer, Puneet, Sushil, Arvind and Harish Rawat used to stay at the servant quarters of the Vithal Bhai Patel House, Rafi Marg.

They used to fight on several occasions over petty issues, the police said.

Harish Rawat was allegedly attacked by his neighbours after a quarrel on Monday, the police said.

Police received an information about the incident around 8:30pm.

He was rushed to the Ram Manohar Lohia Hospital, where he was discharged after treatment, they said.

All the three accused were arrested and a case under the section 307 (attempt to murder) of the Indian Penal Code has been registered against them, the police said, adding that one blood-stained wooden stick was recovered from their possession.
