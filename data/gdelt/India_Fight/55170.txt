Hold your breath! Delhi's air turns 'hazardous' as AQI reaches 407

Key Highlights On Thursday morning, the AQI of Delhi was recorded as 407 AQI between 401 and 500 is said to be 'hazardous' category On Wednesday, the AQI of the national capital was 360

New Delhi: The level of air pollution in the national capital saw a considerable increase on Thursday. The overall AQI of Delhi on Thursday morning was 407, according to the System of Air Quality and Weather Forecasting And Research (SAFAR). The air quality of Delhi has been deteriorating even after the government has taken several steps to curb pollution.

On Wednesday, the AQI of Delhi was recorded as 360. The quality of air, however, worsened on Thursday morning. Initially, SAFAR had predicted that the air quality in the national capital is likely to remain in the 'very poor' category till Thursday (December 12).

Around 7 am on Thursday, Lodhi Road had PM10 pollutants at 261 and PM2.5 pollutants at 385. The air quality of Mathura Road was in the 'severe' category with PM10 pollutants at 424 and PM2.5 pollutants at 438.

Delhi's famous Chandni Chowk had PM10 pollutants at 448 and PM2.5 pollutants at 445. The quality of air in Pusa improved slightly as PM10 pollutants reached 279 and PM2.5 pollutants were recorded as 388.

Noida, Uttar Pradesh battled with poor air quality as PM10 pollutants were recorded at 429 and PM2.5 pollutants stood at 404. Meanwhile, Ghaziabad's Vasundhara had an AQI of 453.

AQI between the range of 51 and 100 is said to be 'satisfactory', 101-200 is termed as 'moderate', 201-300 falls under the category of 'poor'. While 300-400 is said to be 'very poor', levels between 401-500 fall under the 'hazardous' category.

The air quality of the national capital has been deteriorating even after the collective efforts of several state governments and the Delhi government to curb pollution. Last month, the Supreme Court had put a ban on construction in Delhi and had slammed the government for not presenting an efficient road-map to curb pollution.
