Abhinandan Varthaman: Story of IAF pilot's bravery will now be part of school syllabus in Rajasthan

Rajasthan

Abhinandan Varthaman

Pakistan

Balakot

Students studying in's schools will soon get to know the story of the bravery shown by Indian Air Force (IAF) fighter pilotA chapter on the valour of Varthaman will be included in textbooks."Our forces keep us safe while sacrificing their own lives, to inspire the upcoming generation about the bravery of our forces, a direction has been given to include Wing Commander Abhinandan's saga of bravery in textbooks," said Rajasthan Education Minister Govind Singh Dotasra.The chapter will include details about how he was in's custody and how he returned to Indian soil.However, the government has not mentioned which classes will have textbooks with the chapter on the IAF pilot.Varthaman recently shot down a Pakistani F-16 warplane before his MiG 21 was downed in a dog fight between the two countries. He was forced to eject from his plane in Pakistani territory.He was returned by Pakistan three days later as a gesture of 'peace', amid ongoing tensions between the two nuclear neighbours.Tensions escalated between India and Pakistan in the wake of the Pulwama terror attack in Kashmir which killed 40 CRPF personnel and was claimed by Pakistan-based terror group Jaish-e-Mohammad (JeM).After the suicide attack, India carried out air strikes against the biggest training camp of JeM inin Pakistan on February 26. The next day, Pakistan Air Force retaliated and downed a MiG-21 in an aerial confrontation.
