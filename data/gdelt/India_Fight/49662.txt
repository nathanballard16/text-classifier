5 workers killed by militants in Indian Kashmir

Srinagar: At least five workers were killed and another was injured in a militant attack in Indian-administered Kashmir, in one of the deadliest attacks in the troubled region in recent months.

The five men, all construction workers from the West Bengal state in eastern India, were shot dead in the southern Kulgam district on Tuesday evening, local police officers said.

The militants abducted the workers, lined them up and later opened fire, killing the five on scene, the Times of India newspaper reported, citing sources.

One worker was critically injured and left for dead. He was takien to a hospital in the regional capital, Srinagar.

“We are shocked and deeply saddened at the brutal killings in Kashmir. All help will be extended to the families (of the victims) in this tragic situation,” West Bengal chief minister Mamata Banerjee said on Twitter. (DPA)
