50 students, teachers trapped as Pakistan shells villages, border outposts in J&K’s Rajouri

JAMMU: About 50 students and teachers of a government school in Nowshera sector of J&K’s Rajouri district were trapped inside the premises on Wednesday as cross-border violence continued unabated amid heightened tension between India and Pakistan. On Tuesday, an Indian Army jawan was injured when Pakistan violated ceasefire and resorted to unprovoked firing and heavy shelling along the Line of Control (LoC) in Rajouri and Poonch districts.“Around 10.30am on Wednesday, Pakistan initiated unprovoked firing of small arms and heavy mortar shelling in Rajouri’s Nowshera and Sunderbani sectors. Indian Army retaliated effectively. The firefight continued till 2pm, and no injury or casualty was reported from the Indian side,” defence spokesperson Lt Col Devender Anand said. Police sources said the firing affected Kalal, Baba Khori and Magyote regions of Nowshera.Around 50 people, including students who had come for their annual examination and teachers of a school in Nowshera’s Kalal area, were trapped inside the premises when Pakistan violated ceasefire. Police said the school children and staff remained indoors as a preventive measure and were rescued after the firing and shelling stopped.On Tuesday, Pakistan breached truce thrice, attacking border outposts and civilian areas in Rajouri and Poonch districts. “Vaibhav Yadav of the 24 Bn of Maratha Light Infantry, who was manning a forward post in Kalal area, got injured in the firing and was shifted to the army hospital in Jammu’s Satwari,” said a source.
