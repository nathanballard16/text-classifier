Key Highlights Sheila Dikshit breathed her last at Delhi's Fortis Escorts Heart Institute on Saturday She was hospitalised after a critical cardiac arrest A life-long Congress leader, Dikshit had a prolific 35-year-long political career

New Delhi: Three-time Delhi chief minister Sheila Dikshit was passed away on Saturday (today) at the age of 81. She breathed her last 3.55 pm in Delhi’s Fortis Escorts Heart Institute. She was admitted to the hospital earlier today (July 20) in in a critical condition with cardiac arrest, the hospital said.

She was the longest serving chief minister of Delhi who held the national capital’s top job for 15 years (1998-2013). She was born on 31 March 1938 in Punjab’s Kapurthala.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi, President Ram Nath Kovind and numerous opposition leaders including Congress party’s former national president Rahul Gandhi, Priyanka Gandhi Congress general secretary for UP (East) and others condoled Sheila Dikshit’s demise.
