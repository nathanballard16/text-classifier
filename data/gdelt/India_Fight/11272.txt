Dalit couple visits temple under police protection; 'He's first in community to enter temple': groom's brother

Police had made adequate security arrangements for the marriage procession and his temple visit. | Photo Credit: Getty

Bhopal: In a case reported from Indore district of Madhya Pradesh, a Dalit bridegroom visited a Ram temple under police protection on Thursday. Reportedly, the members of the upper castes allegedly did not allow the Dalit community to enter the Ram temple at Aurangpura village.

The news agency PTI reported, 22-year-old, Ajay Malviya's marriage procession was taken out in police protection and extra security was also provided when he visited the temple.

The Sub-Divisional Officer of Police (SDOP) Ram Kumar Rai told the agency that they had made adequate security arrangements for the marriage procession and his temple visit. He further added that there was no untoward incident. Meanwhile, the Akhil Bhartiya Balai Mahasabha had submitted an application to the Betma police station where they alleged that Dalits were not allowed to enter the Ram temple at Aurangpura.

It also alleged that caste-based discrimination was prevalent in the village. Alleging that there had been trouble during the marriage procession of another Dalit man in the past.

Reportedly, the organisation had been demanded security for Malviya's wedding procession. PTI reported, Dharmendra Malviya the groom's elder brother said he has not seen in his entire life any person belonging to his community entering the Ram temple. Dharmendra also said that when he rode a horse during his marriage procession in 2009, stones were pelted at them.
