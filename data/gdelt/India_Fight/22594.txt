‘Khatara car’ taunt leads to fight with guns, two shot

GREATER NOIDA: Three persons were injured in two incidents of firing reported from two villages in Badalpur area on Saturday.Shots were fired as two groups of villagers from Kachaida and Duryayi fought with each other. Police said Kachaida resident Sunil’s car had broken down on Saturday evening and he was taking it to the village after tying it to another a vehicle belonging to one Shakti Singh.When they reached near Duryayi, a group led by Sanjay Sharma and his friends allegedly started taunting them and called the car “khatara” (scrap). Sunil said when he and Shakti resisted, the youths started hitting them.“Soon, two residents from Kachaida, Yogesh and Dinesh, who were returning from Ghaziabad joined in. One person apparently fired in the air when the fight got serious,” a Kachaira resident said. Yogesh was shot in the thigh and another person received bullet injuries. Six others, including Dinesh, Shakti and Sunil, were also injured.Vinay Kumar, SHO, Badalpur police station, said an FIR has been lodged against eight people, most of them from Duryayi, under IPC sections 323 (voluntarily causing hurt), 307 (attempt to murder), 342 (wrongful confinement), 147 (rioting), 148 (rioting armed with deadly weapon) and 149 (unlawful assembly guilty of offence committed in prosecution of common object). At least five people from Duryayi have been detained.In another incident, a resident Bambawad village was shot at by two bike-borne men on Saturday morning. Sunil Nagar (40) was shot at while he was on his way to his farm. A bleeding Sunil drove back to his house and his relatives took him to a private clinic.Later, he was taken to Fortis Hospital in Sector 62. Sunil’s son Anuj told TOI that the incident happened around 8 am. “He drove back home and lost consciousness soon after,” Anuj said, adding that Sunil is at the ICU. An FIR has been lodged under IPC Section 307 (attempt to murder) at Badalpur police station.
