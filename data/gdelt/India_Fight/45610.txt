Article 370: Supreme Court To Begin Hearing On Pleas Challenging Abrogation, Restrictions Today

A 5-judge Constitution bench of the Supreme Court will start hearing pleas challenging the legal validity of the Centre's abrogation of the provision of Article 370 that gave special status to Jammu and Kashmir. Another 3-judge Kashmir bench led by Justice N V Ramana will take up individual petitions on alleged restrictions imposed in the Valley and lack of access to basic facilities. The Centre on August 5 moved to end special status to Jammu and Kashmir and bifurcated the region into two union territories of Jammu and Kashmir and Ladakh. A day later, Parliament passed the changes in the form Jammu and Kashmir Reorganization Act, 2019. The UTs will come into being on October 31. Read more…
