Vicky Kaushal film Uri tax-free in Uttar Pradesh, announces Yogi Adityanath

Vicky Kaushal and Yami Gautam starrer Uri - The Surgical Strike has been declared free of tax by the Yogi Adityanath-led Uttar Pradesh government today. The Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister, speaking at a cabinet meeting at Prayagraj, announced that Uri would be made free of the state GST in Uttar Pradesh.

Uri has already earned Rs 157.38 crore in just seventeen days of its release. The film, directed by Aditya Dhar, is based on 2016 Uri attack by armed militants near the town of Uri in Jammu and Kashmir and India's retaliation, the surgical strike.

The film has been lauded by critics and the audience alike. In fact, recently, the tagline of the film, 'How's The Josh' went viral and even Prime Minister Narendra Modi too could not help but quote it at the inaugural event of the National Museum of Indian Cinema in Mumbai.

The video of him asking the crowd, 'How's The Josh' went viral. The video showed the crowd responding, "High sir!" The audience at the event mostly consisted of people from the film fraternity. Modi spoke about the importance of cinema in India, and said that films and society are a reflection of each other and like cinema, India too, is changing with the times at the event.
