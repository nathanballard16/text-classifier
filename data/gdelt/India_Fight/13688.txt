'India belongs to citizens from every corner': Rahul Gandhi hails man who saved Kashmiris from attackers in Lucknow

Presidenton Friday hailed the person who saved Kashmiri vendors from being beaten-up inand said that the country belongs to citizens from every corner.Gandhi's reaction comes after two Kashmiri vendors selling dry fruits were thrashed by a group of saffron-clad men in Lucknow on Wednesday.Condemning the, Gandhi tweeted a video of the incident and said, "While I am disgusted by this video of Kashmiri traders being attacked in UP, I salute the braveheart who challenged the attackers."The Congress leader further stated that India belongs to citizens from every corner of our nation and he strongly condemn all acts of violence against our Kashmiri brothers and sisters.The police have arrested four people in connection with the incident. The accused have been identified as Bajrang Sonkar, Amar, Himanshu and Anirrudh.Sonkar, who was arrested earlier, is affiliated to the Vishwa Hindu Dal (VHD) and has a dozen criminal cases registered against him including one of murder.Police said that the attackers were initially booked under the Indian Penal Code, but non-bailable offences have also been included now.The victims - Abdul Salam and Mohammed Afzal Nayak - have been assured of security to carry out their business and have been provided medical care and compensation.There have been reports from different parts of the country targeting Kashmiris in the aftermath of the Pulwama terror attack that killed more than 40 CRPF personnel.
