Four dead in gas plant fire in western India

New Delhi: At least four people were killed in a fire at a state-run oil and gas processing plant in western India on Tuesday, police said.

The fire started in a stormwater drain at the Oil and Natural Gas Corporation (ONGC) plant in Uran, some 45 kms south of Mumbai, police spokesman Pallab Bhattacharya said.

The blaze was brought under control within about two hours. Three people were injured but they were not in critical condition. (DPA)
