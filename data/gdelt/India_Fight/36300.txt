Assam flood situation improves but rivers flow above danger mark

GUWAHATI: Although flood-situation in Assam showed improvement, 5 more bodies were recovered from across different districts on Friday. This took the final toll to 81.The overall situation was considerably better with the number of people affected coming down to 27, 15, 184 on Friday from Thursday's 34,92,734. Flood-hit districts also came down to 17 from Thursday's 18. Croplands spanning over 1,22,835.80 hectares remained inundated.According to the Assam State Disaster Management Authority (ASDMA) report, water levels of the Brahmaputra at Neamatighat (Jorhat) and Dhubri, the Jia Bharali river in Sonitpur, the Puthimari river in Kamrup and the Beki in Barpeta remained abover danger level. Flood situation in lower Assam districts had aggravated on Thursday after excess water was released from Kuricchu hydropower dam on Beki river in Bhutan. Flood waters rose in Barpeta, Nalbari, Baksa, Chirang, Kokrajhar, Dhubri and South Salmara following release of this excess water from the Kuricchu dam.Barpeta remained the worst-hit with over 10.71 lakh people affected. 1,58,896 flood-displaced people have taken shelter in 768 relief camps set up in Baksa, Nalbari, Barpeta, Chirang, Bongaigaon, Kokrajhar, Dhubri, Goalpara, Kamrup, Morigaon, Nagaon, Golaghat and Jorhat districts. Also 161 relief distribution centres are functioning in Dhemaji, Lakhimpur, Barpeta, Bongaigaon, Kokrajhar, Kamrup and Morigaon districts.Bongaigaon district administration also informed that water level of three major rivers - Aie, Manas and Brahmaputra - were below danger level. "At present there are approximately 1,87,942 flood-affected people coming down from 2,52, 850 was on Thursday. There are 13 relief camps in Bijni, 59 relief centres in Srijangram, and nine relief camps in Boitamari in Bongaigaon, providing services to 13,542 flood-affected inmates and other stranded people," the administration said.
