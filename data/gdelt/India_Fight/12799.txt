NEW DELHI: Equity benchmarks Sensex and Nifty logged strong gains on Tuesday on healthy buying in bank, financial, auto, oil & gas and metal stocks.Investors’ risk appetite got a boost after crude oil prices softened further, the rupee gained strength against the US dollar and analysts projected improved prospects for Prime Minister Modi in the forthcoming general elections.Expectations for an increase in the US crude inventories dragged oil price down towards the $65 a barrel mark. Resumption of Libya's biggest oilfield added more pressure on the commodity price.Sensex closed the day 379 points, or 1.05 per cent, higher at 36,442, with 24 stocks ending in the green and seven in the red. Nifty50 settled 124 points, or 1.14 per cent, higher at 10,987, with 39 stocks advancing and 11 declining.Midcaps and smallcaps outperformed Sensex with wider margins; the BSE Midcap index rose 2.01 per cent and the BSE Smallcap index 3.11 per cent.Let's take a look at the key highlights of Tuesday's session:Tuesday's rally made investors richer by Rs 2.45 lakh crore, as the total market capitalisation of BSE-listed firms shot up to Rs 1,44,27,254.47 crore from Rs 1,41,81,544.79 crore on Friday.As many as 304 stocks, including Tata Steel, Graphite India, Vakrangee, Reliance Naval and Engineering, Shankara Building Products and IL&FS Transportation Networks, hit upper circuit limits. However, Jaypee Infratech, Gyscoal Alloys, Industrial Investment Trust, Eurotex Industries & Exports and Oasis Tradelink were among the 177 stocks that touched their lower circuits. Repco Home Finance, with a change of 84 per cent, led a pack of stocks that witnessed the biggest spurt in open interest (OI) on NSE. It was followed by Ujjivan Financial Services (50 per cent), PC Jeweller (38.45 per cent), Infibeam Avenues (38.17 per cent) and(36.89 per cent).Shares of Tata Motors closed 7.72 per cent higher at Rs 194 after its subsidiary JLR reported better-than-expected retail volume for February in the US market. The carmaker said volumes jumped 26 per cent on a YoY basis, which was higher than 18-20 per cent anticipated by Street. Tata Group's move to rejig the $104-billion conglomerate into 10 verticals was also seen as a positive for the company.The Reserve Bank of India has slapped a fine of Rs 1 crore on YES Bank for non-compliance of directions on the Swift messaging software, the global messaging software used for transactions by the financial entities. The Rs 14,000 crore fraud at the PNB was a case of misuse of this massaging software. Shares of YES Bank closed 0.04 per cent down at Rs 237.30.Shares of Cadila Healthcare closed 2.48 per cent up at Rs 331.20 after it received the USFDA approval to market Rivastigmine Transdermal System, marking its first approval for a transdermal product in the US.Shares of GATI jumped 1.90 per cent to close at Rs 80.35 after reports emerged that the company's promoters may sell their entire stake to TVS Logistics. An ETNow report suggested that a deal between Gati and TVS Logistics may be announced soon and other investors may also decide to exit their stake.Shares of Aurobindo Pharma climbed 1.73 per cent to settle at Rs 735.90 after the company said it completed the acquisition of seven marketed oncology injectable products from Spectrum Pharmaceuticals Inc.Momentum indicator moving average convergence divergence, or MACD, showed bullish crossovers on 130 counters on BSE, indicating that these stocks may rally in the coming sessions. Among them were Adani Ports,, Jammu & Kashmir Bank,, Eicher Motors, SRG Housing Finance and Atul. Meanwhile, Bata India, Kansai Nerolac Paint, PTC Industries and Endurance Technologies were among the 17 stocks that showed bearish crossovers, signalling possibility of drop in these stocks.Energy (up 25.04 per cent), Repco Home Finance (up 20.70 per cent), IVP (up 20 per cent), BGR Energy (up 20 per cent), Vaswani Industries (up 20 per cent), Wheels India (up 19.99 per cent), HIM Teknoforge (up 19.97 per cent), Vinyl Chemicals (up 19.96 per cent) and Oswal Agro Mill (up 19.95 per cent) were among the stocks that surged up to 25 per cent on Tuesday.Punjab National Bank (PNB), Indian Oil Corporation (IOC), NCC, BPCL, HDIL, Blue Star, NMDC, JSW Energy, Bosch and Bank of India were among the stocks that witnessed positive breakouts and traded above their 200-DMAs. Meanwhile, Endurance Technologies, Manaksia Aluminium Company, Bombay Super Hybrid Seeds, Uttam Value Steels and Supreme Industries were among the stocks that slipped below their 200-DMAs.
