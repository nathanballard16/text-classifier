CBI registers 12 new cases against Chhota Rajan, associates over extortion, kidnapping

In August, Chhota Rajan and five others were sentenced to prison for eight years by a special court in Mumbai. (File Photo)

The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) on Friday registered 12 new cases against underworld don Chotta Rajan and his associates on charges including extortion, kidnapping and possession of illegal weapons.

The 12 new cases are are part of the 71 cases transferred from Mumbai police to the CBI on November 21, 2015.

In August, Chhota Rajan and five others were sentenced to prison for eight years by a special court in Mumbai in a 2012 attempt-to-murder and extortion case lodged against them by a Mumbai hotelier.

The court had convicted Chhota Rajan and five others under the Arms Act. Apart from a prison sentence, the court has also asked the underworld don to pay a fine of Rs 5 lakh.

Rajan is currently lodged in Tihar jail in Delhi after his deportation to India following his arrest in Indonesia in October 2015.
