100 booked for violence at Maharashtra rally

NEW DELHI: The Maharashtra police have booked nearly 100 people in an attempt-to-murder and rioting case for Friday’s violence during an anti-CAA rally in Beed . Police have also decided to deny permission to any rally related to CAA. A group of about 2,000 people had gathered in Bashirgunj area on Friday. They were going towards the district collector’s office.As the rally passed the Shivaji Statue area, protesters started pelting stones at two state transport buses that left some cops injured. The police resorted to lathi charge and used teargas to bring the situation under control. SP Harsh Poddar said, “We have arrested 30 people. Others will be arrested soon.”
