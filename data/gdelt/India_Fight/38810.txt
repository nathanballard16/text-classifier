Cash looted from priest's house in Jalandhar: Four cops dismissed

PATIALA: Almost four months after the Khanna police landed in a soup for carrying out a raid at the house of Jalandhar based priest and taking away Rs 16.65 crores from his house out of which Rs 9.66 crore went missing, the state police on Saturday dismissed the services of four cops including three local rank assistant sub inspectors and a head constable, who were a part of the raiding team.The action has been taken on the directions of the director general of police, Dinkar Gupta , on the basis of a departmental inquiry conducted by the Patiala senior superintendent of police MS Sidhu. Confirming the development Sidhu said action against local rank ASIs-Joginder Singh, Rajpreet Singh and Dilbagh Singh- along with head constable Amrik Singh had been taken under Section 311(2) (B) of the Indian Constitution.The Four cops are presently lodged at the Patiala Central Jail and are facing charges in the said case. While ASIs Joginder Singh and Rajpreet Singh had gone missing with the cash 'looted' from the priest’s house, Dalbir Singh and Amrik Singh had been arrested for helping them.The police had constituted an SIT to crack the case which arrested 10 persons including three ASIs, the head constable. police informer Surinder Singh, Nirmal Singh of Raipur village in Mansa, who is the maternal uncle of ASI Rajpreet from whom some cash had been recovered, Surinderpal alias Chiri of Patran Mohammad Shakeel neighbour of ASI Joginder Singh and Devinder Kumar of Moonak and one Sanjeev Kumar had been arrested by the SIT.It was on March 29, this year, that the Khanna police carried out a raid at the Partappura village residence of Father Antony Madasserry, a key functionary of Diocese of Jalandhar that led to the seizure Rs 16.65 crore in cash. The next day Khanna SSP held a presser and claimed they had recovered Rs 9.66 crore of alleged hawala money during checking near Doraha. However, the next day, the priest held a press briefing at Jalandhar and alleged the police had taken away a sum of Rs 16.65 crore and "misappropriated Rs9 .66 crore.'On April 1, Punjab DGP ordered an inquiry and later constituted an SIT under IGP Crime Parveen Sinha, which cracked the case.On April 30, the SIT arrested the two absconding ASI from Kochi which led to additional arrests and the recovery of Rs 4.49 crore out of Rs 6.65 crore. However, there is still uncertainty about the total cash that had been taken away from the priest’s house, who according to the police, is yet to furnish the complete documents related to the total cash.
