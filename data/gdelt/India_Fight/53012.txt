Mumbai: CBI to probe union leader Datta Samant murder case

MUMBAI: The CBI has taken over the investigation into the killing of trade union leader Datta Samant in 1997 in Mumbai, in which the role of jailed gangster Chhota Rajan is suspected, officials said on Thursday. This is among the 10 cases registered by the probe agency pertaining to alleged involvement of Rajan in cases of extortion, murder and threats among others over a 14-year period since 1992, they said.Samant, a trade union leader, was shot dead on January 16, 1997, when he was travelling in his Jeep towards his office in Pant Nagar. The assailants, who came on a bike, had fired 17 bullets, killing the labour leader responsible for the 1981 Mumbai textile mill workers’ year-long strike.A case was registered by the city police and it was handed over to the CBI in 2015, with 70 other cases after Rajan’s capture in Indonesia and his deportation to India.One of the alleged shooters, Vijaykumar Chaudhary, was killed in an encounter with Mumbai police in 2007 in Kolhapur. As per procedure, the CBI has taken over the FIR of Mumbai police registered on the basis of the statement of Samant’s driver and re-registered as its case. Other cases registered by the CBI pertain to extortion, Bhandup rioting and murder in 1992, and firing and murder bid on an Andheri resident in 2004.
