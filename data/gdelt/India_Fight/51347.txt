Woman Falls From Scooter In Front Of Truck Trying To Avoid An AIADMK Flagpole

There was outrage in the country when a 23-year-old techie in Chennai was killed after an illegal hoarding of an AIADMK leader fell on her, following which she was hit by a water tanker.

Now another incident of political signs causing harm to people has come up, and again in Tamil Nadu. Rajeswari, a 30-year-old woman from Coimbatore was left terribly injured after she came under a truck while she was trying to avoid a flag pole of the ruling All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK).

Twitter

The woman fell from her scooter when he was trying to avoid the party’s flag post that almost fell in her path. The flag post was erected on the side of Avinashi Road near Goldwins bus stop. In the process, she lost her balance and she along with her vehicle fell right in front of an approaching truck.

The victim received multiple fractures and her legs were crushed. The police have filed an FIR against the lorry driver.

Techie who died in September/Twitter

Sadly, this incident occurred the same day when Madras High Court granted conditional bail to to the main accused in the death of the 23-year-old Subhashree, AIADMK leader Jayagopal. He was absconding for sometime after the outrage over the young techie’s death took place and was only arrested recently.

The Madras High Court put a ban on banners but when asked about the recent incident Tamil Nadu Chief Minister Edappadi K Palaniswami said that no restrictions on putting up flagpoles have been proposed.
