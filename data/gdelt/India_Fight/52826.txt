Three held on religious conversion charge

VARANASI: The police arrested three persons at Daraura village in Ranipur area of Mau district on Tuesday on charges of attempting to convert some people to Christianity.Inspector Ranipur Ashok Yadav said, “Three persons — one Kapildev and priests Ajay Kumar and Om Prakash— have been arrested on charges of trying to induce some people to Christianity.”“The arrested persons have been booked under sections 153A (promoting enmity between different groups on grounds of religion, race, place of birth, residence, language, etc., and doing acts prejudicial to maintenance of harmony), 295A (deliberate and malicious acts, intended to outrage religious feelings of any class by insulting its religion or religious beliefs), 508 (act caused by inducing person to believe that he will be rendered an object of the divine displeasure) of IPC and 3/9 of Religious Institutions (Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1988),” he added.According to reports, supporters of some Hindutva organizations assembled at the place of one Kapildev Ram after getting information of alleged conversion. The police also reached there after getting information and found some religious literature.Meanwhile, some persons were also brought to police station in Nawadhia area of Jaunpur district on Wednesday for holding alleged conversion programme, but they were let off after police found the allegation false.Newadhia Inspector Raj Narayan Chaurasia said, “The police raided the place after getting information of mass conversion. But, it was false information, as there is no restriction in holding prayer meeting.”
