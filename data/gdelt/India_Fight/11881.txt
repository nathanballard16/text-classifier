Two soldiers and two civilians killed in Indian firing across LoC, says Pakistan

Two soldiers had been killed while returning fire to Indian posts firing across the Line of Control (LoC).

According to a statement issued to Dawn by ISPR soldiers were killed at Nakiyal Sector in exchange of fire while targeting Indian posts undertaking firing on civilian population,” the ISPR statement read.

“Those who were killedinclude Havaldar Abdur Rub and Naik Khuram.”

“Reports of casualties of Indian troops and damage to post due to effective response by Pakistan Army,” it concluded.

Earlier, ISPR had reported Indian firing across the LoC, as a result of which two citizens were killed while two others, including a woman, were injured.

Separately, a 19-year-old youth, identified as Abdul Ghaffar, was injured in Darra Sher Khan when he was shot at by an Indian sniper from across the LoC.

“Indian troops undertook firing along LoC. Exchange of fire in Hotspring, Tatta Pani and Jandrot Sectors where India also deliberately targeted civil population,” the ISPR statement read.

The injured civilians have been evacuated to a hospital in Kotli.
