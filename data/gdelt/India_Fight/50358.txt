CBI raids textile baron, others in Ludhiana

LUDHIANA: In one of the biggest ever action by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) in Ludhiana, raids were conducted at several premises belonging to prominent city businessmen by different teams of the economic offences branch of CBI’s Delhi headquarters. The raids are said to be part of the CBI’s nationwide raids at more than 180 locations in connection with the Rs 7,200 crore banking fraud.One of the groups raided in today’s action is the textile conglomerate which is one of the leading textile manufacturers of the country and has been into controversy from quite sometime now after being involved into alleged Rs 113.55 crore fraud with Bank of Maharashtra and CBI has been probing this. In addition to this group the CBI team also raided a house in Sarabha Nagar , which allegedly belongs to a prominent liquor trader of Ludhiana and interestingly is located near the residence of owners of textile company.Later in the day a statement was issued by CBI, which said, “In a large scale operation against frauds in banks, the CBI is today conducting searches at around 187 places in different states/UTs including Andhra Pradesh , Chandigarh, Delhi, Gujarat, Haryana, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Punjab, Tamil Nadu, Telangana , Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Dadra & Nagar Haveli related to bank fraud cases. CBI has registered around 42 cases of bank frauds involving Rs 7,200 crore (approx.). The banks involved (including consortium members) are Andhra Bank, Oriental Bank of Commerce, Indian Overseas Bank, SBI, Allahabad Bank , Canara Bank, Dena Bank , Punjab & Sind Bank, Punjab National Bank, Central Bank of India, Union Bank of India, IDBI Bank, Bank of Baroda, Bank of Maharashtra and Bank of India."
