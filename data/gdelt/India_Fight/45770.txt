ISRO Scientist Allegedly Murdered Inside His Hyderabad Flat, Was Hit On Head With Heavy Object

In a shocking incident, a scientist working with the National Remote Sensing Centre (NRSC) of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was found brutally murdered in his apartment.

According to IANS , the scientist named S Suresh was allegedly killed by unknown persons at his flat at Annapurna Apartment in Ameerpet area in Hyderabad.

Suresh, a native of Kerala, was alone in his flat. His friends and colleagues sensed something amiss when he did not come to office on Tuesday. Suresh has been living in Hyderabad for 20 years. His wife was also working in the city but was transferred to Chennai in 2005. Their son is settled in the US, while the daughter lives in New Delhi.

When his colleagues called him on his mobile number, there was no response. They alerted his wife Indira, who is a bank employee in Chennai.

Soon after, Suresh's wife along with some other family members rushed to Hyderabad and approached the police. They broke open the flat to find Suresh lying dead.

Police suspect that he was hit on the head with a heavy object, resulting in his death and body was shifted for autopsy.

“There were three injuries on the back of his head,” said S Murali Krishna, SR Nagar police inspector to the Hindu.

Senior police officials visited the scene and a team gathered clues. They said they were scanning the CCTV footage of the apartment complex to probe the case.
