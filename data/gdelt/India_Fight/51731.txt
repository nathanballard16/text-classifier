Allahabad: Niranjani Akhara seer kills self over ill health

ALLAHABAD: A 40-year-old seer associated with Panchayati Akhara Shri Niranjani ( Niranjani Akhara ) allegedly shot himself dead with his licensed pistol in his room at the akhara’s premises, under the limits of Daraganj police station, on Sunday morning.The deceased, Mahant Ashish Giri, was secretary of the akhara. SSP (Prayagraj) Satyarth Anirudh Pankaj said, “Our preliminary investigation shows that Giri might have taken his own life as he was fed up with his prolonged illness.”Police said Giri was suffering from high blood pressure along with stomach and liver ailments. The body has been sent for autopsy. The SSP said the time of death was between 8.30am to 9am. “Around 8 am, one of the seers called Giri on his mobile phone and asked him to come for breakfast. Giri told him that he would come after having bath. However, when he did not turn up, his disciples went looking for him in his room and found him lying in a pool of blood,” the SSP said. DIG (Prayagraj range) KP Singh and SP (city) Brijesh Kumar Srivastava rushed to the spot with forensic experts.“We found Giri’s medical reports which show that his liquor addiction had harmed his liver,” SP (city) Srivastava.Giri, a native of Uttarakhand, came to Allahabad in 2011 and was made secretary of Akhara in 2013, he added. Akhil Bharatiya Akhara Parishad (ABAP) president Narendra Giri also rushed to the spot. He told police that Giri had been reeling under depression since past couple of months due to his illness.
