Kamlesh Tiwari murder case: Two accused arrested by Gujarat ATS

Gujarat ATS DIG Himanshu Shukla | Photo Credit: ANI

Surat: In a big development in the Kamlesh Tiwari murder case, Gujarat Anti-Terrorist Squad (ATS) on Tuesday nabbed the two murder accused from Gujarat-Rajasthan border near Shamlaji. The two prime suspects in the murder case carried a reward of Rs 2.5 lakh each.

After the arrest, Gujarat ATS DIG Himanshu Shukla told media, "The two wanted accused Ashfaq and Moinuddin Pathan have been arrested from Gujarat-Rajasthan border near Shamlaji. Gujarat ATS had information that they are going to enter Gujarat, on that basis we moved our team to the border and apprehended them."

Earlier on Monday, Uttar Pradesh Police announced a collective reward of Rs 5 lakh for information leading to the whereabouts of the two accused.

On Saturday, Uttar Pradesh and Gujarat Police have detained five persons in connection with the murder case of Kamlesh Tiwari. Three persons were taken in remand for further interrogation while the remaining two were released.

After detaining the suspects, Uttar Pradesh Police DGP OP Singh revealed that the accused have murdered Kamlesh Tiwari over his objectionable comments on Prophet Muhammad in 2015.

Back on Friday, Kamlesh Tiwari was shot and stabbed multiple times at his Lucknow residence. He was rushed to a nearby hospital where he succumbed to his injuries.
