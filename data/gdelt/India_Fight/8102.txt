Women, children helped terrorists ferry explosives for Pulwama attack: Report

Pulwama terror attack site | Photo Credit: PTI

New Delhi: Agencies probing the deadly Pulwama terror attack have learned that the explosives used to carry out the strike was ferried by terrorists a transit point in Tral in Pulwama district in South Kashmir with the help of women and children. News agency IANS quoted intelligence sources as saying that RDX grade 5, which is meant for military use, and ammonium nitrate were used in the February 14 Pulwama attack in Jammu and Kashmir.

Forty personnel of the paramilitary Central Reserve Police Force were martyred on February 14 when a local suicide bomber, Aadil Dar, rammed his SUV into a CRPF bus which was part of a convoy of over 70 vehicles moving from Jammu to Srinagar. More than 2,500 jawans were travelling in the huge convoy.

According to sources, the trigger used in the attack was manufactured locally. Further, the military grade RDX was brought in from Pakistan and the bomb was assembled some 10 kms from the attack site. The convoy was attacked in Awantipora area of Pulwama district.

The security forces avenged the Pulwama attack just four days later on February 18 by eliminating three Jaish-e-Muhammed (JeM) terrorists just kilometres away from the attack site. Among the three were Kamran, a resident of Pakistan and the mastermind of the Pulwama terror attack.
