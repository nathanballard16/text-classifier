Opposition tells CM Devendra Fadnavis to apologise over calendar issue

MUMBAI: The Maharashtra government came under fire for not mentioning death anniversaries of Dalit icon B R Ambedkar and social reformer Mahatma Jyotiba Phule in its 2019 calendar. The Opposition has sought an apology from chief minister Devendra Fadnavis for the "oversight".Published by Directorate General of Information and Public Relations, it has not mentioned the death anniversaries of Phule and Ambedkar, which fall on November 28 and December 6.DGIPR stated that as per a decision in 2013, only birth anniversaries are celebrated at the government level, and hence, only they are mentioned. It added that earlier, birth anniversaries of national icons were mentioned only if it was a public holiday.NCP state unit president Jayant Patil claimed that even dates of Mahavir Jayanti and Buddha Purnima were mentioned incorrectly. tnn
