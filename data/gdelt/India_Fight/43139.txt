Clashes in Kolkata as BJP youth workers protest against high power tariff; police use water cannons, tear gas

Clashes between police and BJP workers in Kolkata | Photo Credit: ANI

Kolkata: Clashes erupted in Kolkata on Wednesday during a protest march by workers of the Bharatiya Janata Party youth wing. The BJP youth workers were holding a rally against high power tariff in the West Bengal capital. The protesters were demanding a reduction in electricity tariff.

As per the details available, the police used water cannons and tear gas shells at Central Avenue to stop BJP supporters from marching ahead. The BJP youth workers were marching towards the Calcutta Electric Supply Corporation (CESC) headquarters. Electricity to Kolkata as well as nearby areas is supplied by CESC.

Reports said some BJP workers were injured in the clashes between the police and the protesters. Some of the BJP youth workers were also taken into detention.

The rally was, as per reports from the ground, led by BJP state general secretaries Raju Banerjee and Sayantan Basu.

Tension prevailed in the Central Avenue area of Kolkata when the reports last came in.
