JeM training for 'underwater' attack; India prepared: Indian Navy

A group of Pakistan-based terror outfit Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM) is training for an underwater attack against India, however, Indian forces are fully prepared and will not let their plans succeed, Chief of Naval Staff Admiral Karambir Singh said on Monday.

"We have received intelligence that the underwater wing of Jaish-e-Mohammed is being trained for attacks. We are keeping track of it and can give an assurance that we are fully alert to foil any such design," said Admiral Singh.

Speaking at General BC Joshi Memorial lecture programme at Savitribai Phule Pune University in Maharashtra, Navy chief highlighted the challenges faced by the Navy in ensuring Maritime security and assured that coastal security personnel will not allow any intrusion.

Mentioning the deadly 26/11 sea-borne terror attacks on Mumbai in 2008, Admiral Singh said that overall maritime security has been enhanced manyfolds to prevent any such incident in the future.

Terror outfit JeM had carried out the terror attack in Jammu and Kashmir's Pulwama on Februrary 14, killing 40 Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) personnel in it.

The 2008 Mumbai attacks were carried out by another terror outfit Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), who sneaked into Mumbai through a sea route and launched a series of attacks which claimed the lives of over 150 people.
