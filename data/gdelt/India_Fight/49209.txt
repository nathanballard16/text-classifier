'Diwali is sweeter when celebrated with our brave soldiers': PM Modi marks festivities with Indian Army in Rajouri

This is his first visit to the valley after the Centre abrogated Article 370, which granted special status to the erstwhile state of Jammu and Kashmir, on August 5.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Sunday marked the occasion of Diwali with soldiers of the Indian Army posted near the Line of Control (LoC) in Jammu and Kashmir's Rajouri district.

News agency ANI posted photos on social media. "Jammu and Kashmir: Prime Minister Narendra Modi celebrated #Diwali in Rajouri district with Army personnel, today," the agency posted on Twitter.

Jammu and Kashmir: Prime Minister Narendra Modi celebrated #Diwali in Rajouri district with Army personnel, today. pic.twitter.com/PyQZeQO2L1 — ANI (@ANI) October 27, 2019

Later, the Prime Minister himself posted from his official handle on Twitter, "#Diwali is sweeter when celebrated with our brave soldiers."

#Diwali is sweeter when celebrated with our brave soldiers. pic.twitter.com/skO2SfcwJ3 — Narendra Modi (@narendramodi) October 27, 2019

The Prime Minister also distributed sweets among the security forces, marking yet another year of celebrating the occasion with the armed forces of the country.
