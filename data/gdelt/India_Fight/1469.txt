Mumbai: Fire breaks out at plastic factory in Kandivali, 5 fire tenders at the spot

Fire at Kandivali plastic factory in Mumbai (Representative image) | Photo Credit: BCCL

Mumbai: A plastic factory in Kandivali in suburban Mumbai was engulfed in flames after a fire broke out in the building on Friday afternoon. Five fire tenders were present at the spot and efforts were on to douse the blaze. The incident took place at Government Industrial Estate at Charkop naka in Kandivali West, the Times of India reported.

Apart from the fire engines, authorities also rushed one jumbo tanker, one ambulance and water tankers at the spot. As per the daily, fire officials received a call at around 1:30 pm about a fire at the Patel rubber company within the premises.

Meanwhile, local level civic officials and police personnel had also reached the spot, reports said. The cause of the fire was yet to be ascertained and no casualties or injuries had been reported so far.
