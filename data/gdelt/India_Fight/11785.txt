'Confusion on their role in Pulwama attack': Pak FM Shah Mahmood Qureshi says his govt in touch with JeM leadership

Pakistan Foreign Minister said that there is some confusion in terms of JeM's involvement in the Pulwama terror attack because they claim no responsibility.

After Pakistan released captured Indian Air Force pilot Wing Commander Abhinandan Varthaman on Friday, Pakistan Foreign Minister Shah Mahmood Qureshi spoke on the Pulwama terror attack saying that the terror group denies the responsibility for the attack. Speaking on the Pulwama incident during an interview to the BBC, Pakistan Foreign Minister Mahmood Qureshi said that his government is in touch with Jaish leadership through people who are known to them and said that the JeM denies its role in the Pulwama terror attack.

Pakistan Foreign Minister also said that there is some confusion in terms of JeM's involvement in the Pulwama terror attack because they claim no responsibility. On February 14, a suicide bomber had targeted an Indian paramilitary convoy after he blew off an explosive-laden vehicle killing 40 CRPF personnel. JeM had taken responsibility for the attack.

India in retaliation to the Pulwama incident had bombed the largest JeM terror training camp in Balakot, Pakistan killing more than 300 terrorists. It was after IAF air strike targeting the terror group, Pakistan attempted to attack military establishment in India on Wednesday in which they lost one F-16 and India lost its MiG-21 which was being flown by Wing Commander Abhinandan Varthaman. During the combat, he had to eject himself but unfortunately, his parachute landed on Pakistan occupied Kashmir following which he was captured by Pakistani Army.
