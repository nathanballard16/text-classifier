Pakistan violates ceasefire along LoC, civilian injure

Northern Command chief visits Akhnoor sector, reviews operational preparedness

JAMMU: One civilian was injured as violence at the Kashmir border ratcheted up since 3am on Friday, with Pakistani troops resuming heavy shelling across posts in Baramulla district ’s Uri sector near Kamalkote, impacting eight border villages. Pakistan again violated ceasefire along the Line of Control (LoC) twice in the evening, targeting forward posts and civilian areas in four J&K sectors — Nowshera, Mendhar, Balakote and Krishna Ghati.Heavy shelling and firing continued in Krishna Ghati sector till last reports came in.“Around 4.15pm, Pakistan resorted to unprovoked firing of small arms and heavy mortar shelling along the LoC in Nowshera sector of Rajouri district ,” defence spokesperson Lt Col Devender Anand said. “The next violation took place around 6pm in Mendhar, Balakote and Krishna Ghati sectors of Poonch district. Indian Army retaliated effectively,” he added.In view of the tense security situation, district authorities have ordered a temporary closure of all educational institutions located within five kilometres of the LoC and the International Border in Rajouri, Poonch, Jammu and Samba districts till further notification. Residents have been advised to stay indoors.On Thursday, one civilian was killed and three others including an Army jawan were injured in cross-border firing when Pakistan breached truce four times, targeting border outposts and civilian areas in Mendhar, Mankote, Balakote, Khari Karmara and Degwara sectors of Poonch district and Nowshera, Sunderbani sectors of Rajouri district.Jammu: Northern Command Army Commander Lt Gen Ranbir Singh on Friday visited forward posts in J&K’s Akhnoor sector to review the operational preparedness and the current security situation. “He was accompanied by the White Knight Corps Commander, Lt Gen Paramjit Singh,” defence spokesperson Lt Col Devender Anand said.The Northern Command chief was briefed about the current operational situation on the ground, prevailing security scenario and the operational preparedness to deal with the same. He was also informed about the measures taken to tackle the increased ceasefire violations by Pakistan.Lt Gen Ranbir Singh interacted with soldiers and complimented them for their unflinching dedication and selfless service to the nation. He lauded the standard operating procedure instituted by the units and formations to curb casualties due to ceasefire violations and infiltration bids.
