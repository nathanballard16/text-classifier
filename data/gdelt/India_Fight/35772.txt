Ravi Shankar Prasad: Ravi Shankar Prasad orders high-level inquiry into MTNL, BSNL fire incidents, Telecom News, ET Telecom

NEW DELHI: Telecom minister Ravi Shankar Prasad has ordered a high-level inquiry into fire incidents in the BSNL and MTNL buildings in Delhi, Mumbai and Kolkata owing to two major fire incidents that occurred at MTNL Bandra, Mumbai and in BSNL building at Salt Lake, Kolkata on Monday.A minor fire incident also occurred in MTNL building at Kidwai Bhawan, New Delhi on the same day. No casualties of any of the employees or other persons were reported from any of the incidents. The mentioned fire outbreaks were controlled by the respective fire departments.The Department of Telecom ( DoT ) has also ordered that BSNL and MTNL should conduct fresh fire and safety audit of each of their buildings or installations in a time bound manner.DoT in a statement said that expert teams from BSNL and MTNL have been deployed round the clock to ensure complete restoration of network and services at the affected premises. Moreover, the situation is being closely monitored at CMD level in both organisations.At the MTNL Bandra office, fire broke out at around 3 PM on Monday and was completely controlled in about 12 hours. As reported by MTNL, the last fire audit of the building was conducted on July 12, 2018 and the building was found to be compliant.Due to the incident, land line services of about 25000 customers and internet services of about 8000 customers are down, which are likely to be restored within next four days.Fire at MTNL Kidwai office broke out at around 4 AM on Monday which was completely brought under control by 7.45 AM. As reported by MTNL, the last fire audit of the building was conducted on May 27, 2019 and the building was found to be compliant.All telecom services from the premises are continuing unaffected by this fire incident, DoT said in a statement.BSNL building at Salt Lake, Kolkata caught fire at 7 PM on Monday which was brought under control before 1:30 AM. All mobile services linked to this installation were restored, except for Andaman & Nicobar Islands and data services for West Bengal. Mobile services in these areas are likely to be restored in the next twenty-four hours.
