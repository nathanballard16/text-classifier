Kashmir braced for violence before Eid festival

Protests broke out in Kashmir after direct rule was imposed by Narendra Modi’s government last week

Fears of renewed violence are rising in Indian-controlled Kashmir as the region braces itself for the Eid festival today after a weekend of protests.

Demonstrations after Friday prayers led to clashes with Indian security forces. India’s only Muslim-majority state has been under virtual martial law since the Hindu nationalist government of Narendra Modi revoked its statehood last week, imposing direct rule.

Thousands of extra troops have locked down the main city, Srinagar, and a blackout of mobile phone and internet networks has left Kashmir cut off from the world for the past week. With supplies of cash, food and medicine running out, thousands have fled, while those left behind struggle to get news of loved ones in or out of the region.
