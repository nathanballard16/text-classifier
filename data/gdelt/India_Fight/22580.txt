Allahabad HC stays arrest of BHU chief proctor in student’s murder

ALLAHABAD: The Allahabad high court has stayed arrest of professor Royana Singh, chief proctor of Banaras Hindu University (BHU), till the submission of a report by police in connection with the murder of Gaurav, a student of the university.However, while staying her arrest, the court refused to quash the FIR lodged against her, observing, “On the basis of the allegations made in the FIR, it cannot be said that no cognizable offence is disclosed against the petitioner.”Disposing of the writ petition filed by Prof Singh, a division bench comprising Justice Pankaj Naqvi and Justice Umesh Kumar directed her (petitioner) to fully cooperate with investigation. The writ petition was filed by the petitioner seeking quashing of the FIR registered on April 3, 2019, at Lanka police station of Varanasi in connection with murder of Gaurav. It was alleged in the FIR that Prof Singh, chief proctor of BHU, conspired to kill Gaurav.It was submitted on behalf of the petitioner that she is chief proctor in the BHU and is responsible for maintaining law and order in the university. Hence, allegations relating to the alleged conspiracy on the part of the petitioner in the said murder case are based on malafide and also are ill-motivated. She had no concern either with the victim or accused, who were suspended students of the BHU. The unfortunate occurrence was an outcome of rivalry between two gangs, she submitted.
