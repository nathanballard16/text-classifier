Army jawan injured on LoC in Pak firing

JAMMU: An army jawan was injured and two heads of cattle were killed as Pakistan troops violated ceasefire along the Line of Control (LoC) and International Border (IB) with unprovoked firing and shelling in Poonch and Kathua districts of Jammu and Kashmir.The Army jawan was injured in Mendhar area of Poonch late on Thursday night. “Our troops effectively responded to the firing,” sources said and added that the injured jawan was evacuated and admitted to hospital.In a separate incident, two cattle heads perished when Pakistan Rangers initiated firing along the IB in Hiranagar sector of Kathua around 9pm on Thursday. “The exchange of fire continued till 4am on Friday,” the sources said.On October 22, naib subedar Sunil Ravsaheb had died when Pakistan troops violated ceasefire along the LoC in Nowshera sector of Rajouri district . The same day, 45-year-old Phool Jan, a resident of Gohald, was injured in another ceasefire violation along the LoC in Poonch district.
