DCW seeks 7 more days to provide home to Unnao rape victim

New Delhi, October 10The Delhi Commission for Women (DCW) on Thursday sought seven days from a city court for providing accommodation to the family of the Unnao rape survivor, who was allegedly raped by expelled BJP MLA Kuldeep Singh Sengar in 2017. The DCW told District Judge Dharmesh Sharma during an in-camera proceeding that they had identified few residential houses for the family and the security personnel but needed some time to finalise the deal, a lawyer privy to the matter said. The court directed the Superintendent of the Jay Prakash Narayan Trauma Centre, AIIMS, Delhi to accommodate the rape survivor and her family at the hostel where they are currently lodged, till further orders, the lawyer said. The court had said the team would assist them in locating and providing suitable place in Delhi at a reasonable rate of rent or in any government accommodation for at least 11 months, he said. The rape survivor’s family had earlier told the court that they wanted to reside in the Capital apprehending threat in Uttar Pradesh, their native state.
